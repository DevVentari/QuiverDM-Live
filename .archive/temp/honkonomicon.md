# Chapter 1: Honktroduction


# Chapter 1: Honktroduction


### Welcome to the


### Welcome to the


### Honkonomicon


### Honkonomicon

Within these pages lies the knowledge of the power of geese
and how to wield it. The Honkonomicon presents plenty of
option both for the DM and the players, designed to ensure
that chaos and havonk will reign supreme, because peace
was definitely never an option.
Chapter 1: Honktroduction
Chapter 1: Honktroduction
In this first chapter you will find informations on how to best
use this book. Following that, the Geese of Power section
goes in depth into the lore and powers of Anserath, the Great
Golden Goose and the Ur-goose, laying the foundation for the
rest of the book.
Chapter 2: Goose Subclasses
Chapter 2: Goose Subclasses
Here you will find eleven subclasses deeply tied to the geese
of power and to goosekind in general. There is one subclass
per each class in the Player's Handbook except for the druid,
that you can find in Circle of the Honk.
Chapter 3: Goosish Magic
Chapter 3: Goosish Magic
The first section, Spells of the Honk, contains sixteen spells
of all levels to enable any spellcaster to wield the power of
geese, while Fowlish Curses proposes seven goosish curses
that follow the same rules of the ones in Van Richten's Guide
to Ravenloft. The last section, Magic Honktems, presents
sixteen magic items of all rarities (from Common to Artifact)
including the Honkonomicon itself.
Chapter 4: Goostiarium
Chapter 4: Goostiarium
The last chapter is all dedicated to the children of the honk:
goose creatures spreading from CR 0 to CR 26, including the
geese of power themselves. Here you will also find some
indications on how to honkify any creature, and the
Adventure Hoonks section with six quest ideas involving
geese, divided per tier of play.

### Other Books


### Other Books

To use this compendium you need the Player's Handbook,
Dungeon Master's Guide and Monster Manual.
You will find references to other books, especially Circle of
the Honk by Vall Syrene. Even if having these books might
give you additional insight or goosish content, you don't need
them to use this compendium.

### Practice Safe Honking


### Practice Safe Honking

Before introducing the materials from the Honkonomicon in
an ongoing campaign, consider the following:
A lot of what you will find in this book can easily introduce
a certain dose of silliness in your games (and we designed
it specifically for this purpose).
Some components of this book (like the crown of the
flaming goose wondrous item) might enable or reinforce
the chance of conflict between playing characters.
Several components of this book (like the call of the Ur-
goose spell) can introduce sudden events or random
effects that can disrupt the regular flow of an adventure.
The main theme of the Honkonomicon is chaos, and we
designed it with that in mind. We love chaos, and we hope
you do too. Nonetheless, consider how the points above will
sit with your group. For example, while most players and
groups enjoy a good joke here and there, this amount of
silliness might not work well with a Curse of Strahd
campaign, and conflict among characters can be an
especially tricky topic for most groups.
It might be worth it to discuss these points ahead openly
during session zero, or to have a new session zero about
them. If you are in doubt between redoing session zero or
not, it's probably better that you do so.
If that's what you need to do, feel free to adapt the
description and lore of what you'll find in this book to better
suit the style and aesthetic of your campaign. The Great
Golden Goose will forgive you.

# Geese of Power


# Geese of Power


### Anserath, the Honking


### Anserath, the Honking


### Chaos


### Chaos

Also known as the Raging Mouths, Demogorgon's Pet and
the Honking Chaos, Anserath is a multi-headed abyssal
horror of terrible proportions. The dissonant honks of its
several avian maws echo through the whole Abyss, making
smaller fiends shiver and powerful demons shake their heads
in concern.
Chaos and Rage
Chaos and Rage
Of the three Geese of Power, Anserath is the one that
embodies chaos in the most destructive way. The chaotic
nature of the Abyss itself flows in its essence, making it
impossible to reliably foresee anything about its behavior. Its
decisions make no sense, and its actions have no purpose
other than delivering wreckage on everything that surrounds
it. At the same time, Anserath is absolutely filled with a kind
of unstoppable, contagious rage that makes it an opponent
impossible to reason with.
Even in the Abyss, other demons mostly treat the Honking
Chaos as something that should be left alone (or to run away
from). It has the habit of randomly appearing on the field of
major battles between demonic armies, causing chaos and
confusion among both sides in completely unforeseeable
ways.
Demon's Pet and Feral Fiend
Demon's Pet and Feral Fiend
Originally, Anserath was created by Pazuzu, flying lord of the
abyssal skies, as a tool to spread extreme destruction and
confusion on its enemies. However, right after being set loose
towards its first battle, the Honking Chaos became
completely impossible to control and went on its own way,
with terrible mayhem for all sides involved.
Right after that, Demogorgon himself claimed Anserath as
his own guardian beast and pet, as the two found significant
kinship in their mutual madness and primordial chaotic
nature. For eons, the Raging Mouths accompanied the Prince
of Demons in battle and kept him company in their shared
lair, to the point of becoming known as Demogorgon's Pet.
However, not even the twin-headed demon lord had the
ability to tame such a creature forever. Eventually, Anserath
ran away from its master to never come back, glad to let loose
its rage on any being encountered without restrictions. It is
said that Demogorgon's paranoia played a role in this
decision, even if Anserath's unpredictable nature might be
enough of an explanation.
In more recent times, the Honking Chaos has been sighted
mostly roaming around Yeenoghu's Realm, especially in the
outskirts of the Seeping Woods. The Prince of Gnolls seems
to be proud to call the demon a guest of its realm, and
formally considers Anserath as a vassal, even if no such
official claim has been made. As opposed to what
Demogorgon did, Yeenoghu seems to understand and respect
the Raging Mouths as a creature of its own: not a pet, but a
savage beast with a wild nature that just can't be tamed.
Yeenoghu's followers are taught to not bother Anserath or
engage it during combat, and to get out of its way when it's
"hunting" (more like "purposelessly destroying"). At the same
time, the troops of the Prince of Gnolls (or the Prince
himself) often follow after Anserath's, taking advantage of the
mayhem left behind while trying to not catch too much of its
attention.
Another location where The Honking Chaos can often be
spotted is the Endless Maze, the layer of the Abyss that
Baphomet claims as his home. The two beast-shaped demon
lords seem to enjoy repeatedly fighting each other, and they
have a relationship that walks the line between friends and
foes.
Cultists of the Honking Chaos
Cultists of the Honking Chaos
Anserath’s followers are typically violent beings who love to
challenge the status quo by using extreme, unmotivated
violence. The demon lord can be a patron for the warlocks of
the pact of the honking chaos (see Chapter 2).
His favorite followers gain the Barbed Tongue trait, the
Fiendish Honk trait, or both.
***Barbed Tongue.*** The creature has a long, barbed tongue that can
be used for unarmed attacks or to grapple a target. The barbed
tongue deals 1d6 piercing damage plus the creature's Dexterity or
Strength modifier and the creature is proficient with it.
***Fiendish Honk (Recharges after a Long Rest).*** This creature
emits an otherworldly honk that strikes fear and confusion in the
hearts of its enemies. Each target chosen by the creature that is
within 30 feet of it and can hear the creature makes a Wisdom
saving throw, with DC 8 + the creature's Charisma modifier + the
creature's proficiency bonus. On a failure, each target becomes
frightened until the end of the target's next turn and uses its
reaction to move at its speed in a random direction.

### The Great Golden Goose


### The Great Golden Goose

Geese are a recurrent presence in several fables and fairy
tales, either physically (like the goose of the golden feathers
or the golden egg) or metaphorically (like Mother Goose and
her nursery rhymes). In these stories geese represent luck,
prosperity and care, but they also embody a "morale of the
story" that rewards the virtuous and punishes the greedy.
These creatures and concepts share the same origin and
inspiration: the Great Golden Goose, a celestial whose
transcendent powers trace back to the dawn of the
multiverse.
Also known as the Mother of Gold, the Cosmic Migrator or
even just "the Goose", she delivers presents and good fortune
to whomever she deems worthy, and fowlish curses to those
who try to exploit the good will of her gifts.
Honkcestral Origins
Honkcestral Origins
Nobody knows who or why the Great Golden Goose was first
created: she simply exists and always existed, being a
manifestation of the multiverse's chaotic necessity to exercise
freedom in the purpose of searching for its own ultimate
meaning. Therefore, the Goose and the egg that created her
always existed, both at the same time. Nobody knows which
one came first, or even if the Goose will ever meet her end.
If the Ur-goose embodies chaos in its raw (and mostly
destructive) state, the Mother of Gold incarnates its softest
facets instead: freedom of body, mind and spirit, and the
chance for any being (even the most unlikely and humble one)
to turn the tides of its own destiny. However, her chaotic
nature can also manifest in the shape of a particularly
vengeful nature, easy to anger and very dangerous, especially
when faced by greed and selfishness or when she needs to
protect her flock.
Migration of the Mind
Migration of the Mind
The Goose's purpose is to search for the most ambitious of
all answers: the meaning of the multiverse. In her quest to
resolve the ultimate question, she periodically visits several
locations of the Upper Planes, the Feywild and the Astral
Sea. Most of the time, her travel is like a cycle that brings her
always to the same locations, making it similar to a
migration. Every once in a while, the Goose might suddenly
change her migration path, visiting other planar destinations
or even the Prime Material. When this happens, her followers
always consider it as a sign that something portentous (either
positive or negative) is about to happen.
Because of her repeated migrations in search for
knowledge and ancestral origin, the Mother of Gold is
considered one of the wisest beings of the multiverse. There
are many that approach her temples, or even the Goose
herself, looking for enlightenment or for answers that can't be
found anywhere else. However, the Goose is mostly enigmatic
and abstracted from reality: she only expresses herself in
divine honks, that need to be interpreted by sages that are
proficient into the fowlish powers.
The Golden Flock
The Golden Flock
The Goose owes the title of "Mother of Gold" both to her
prolificacy and to her nature. All of the geese of the golden
feathers (see Chapter X: Monsters) descend directly from her,
therefore she's responsible for the existence of all of the
golden eggs in the world. Additionally, it is believed that the
Divine Golden Egg (see Chapter X: Magic Items) is none
other than her own original, true egg, or at least an egg
directly laid from her.
In a wider sense, the Mother of Gold considers as part of
her flock any humble creature that has been afflicted by
misfortune but never turned to evil or darkness because of
the hardness of life. When her transcendent sight falls on a
humble spirit that needs help, she might send one of her
geese of the golden feathers to lay one or more golden eggs
for it, or to guide a worthy being to a brighter future.
Even if the Great Golden Goose is not a proper deity, her
transcended enlightenment can act as a medium between
divine powers and the mortals who share her ideals and
nature. Therefore, several temples are built in her name, and
she delivers power to the clerics of the Golden Goose
Domain (see Chapter X).
At the same time, the Mother of Gold is considered an
inspiration by many paladins of the Oath of the Righteous
Gander (see Chapter X) and can act as patron for some
celestial warlocks (see Xanathar's Guide to Everything and
the additional invocations in Chapter X of this book).
Gifts of Gold
Gifts of Gold
Here are some examples of the supernatural gifts that the
Great Golden Goose could deliver to a servant or to a worthy
mortal, and a recommended level for each of them (see
Chapter 7 of the Dungeon Master's Guide for rules about
Supernatural Gifts):
Blessing of Plumage (Level 1-4). A creature you touch
grows golden feathers as if you cast the plumage armor*
spell. Once you do so, you can't do it again until the next
dawn.
Blessing of the Guardian Goose (Level 5-10). A celestial
gander is assigned to look over you, and you can summon it
as an action as if it was the summon goose guardian* spell
(Honking Defender only). Once you do so, you can't do it
again until the next dawn.
Blessing of the Avian Sight (Level 11-16). As an action,
you channel the enigmatic gaze of the Goose as if you casted
the gaze of the golden goose* spell. Once you do so, you can't
do it again until the next dawn.
Blessing of the Golden Quill (Level 17-20). You own a
feather of the Great Golden Goose herself, that you can use
as if it was the power word: quill* spell. Once you do so, the
feather loses all power.

### Ur-goose, Primordial of


### Ur-goose, Primordial of


### Havonk


### Havonk

Ancient entity of chaos, confusion and general mischief, the
Ur-goose is a primordial creature that embodies the more
disorderly aspects of the natural world. It is the most pure
and primal representation of goosedom in the absolute sense.
As druids would teach you, nature knows no ethics or
morality, finding its own purpose in itself: the gentle breeze
that spreads the seeds and the terrifying storms that
eradicate the trees both belong to the same unity, and nature
doesn't perceive one or the other as right or wrong. Disarray
and havoc also have a place in the natural balance, and the
Ur-goose embodies them perfectly.
Wild Goose Chase
Wild Goose Chase
In the time when deities and primordials were at war to
establish who was gonna be the true ruler, the Ur-goose
expressed no interest in that conflict. Instead, it took great
enjoyment into acting mischievously towards both sides,
playing as a wildcard into several battles or just throwing off
everybody's plans for the sake of it.
Following the fall of their enemies, several deities have
been looking for the Primordial of Havonk in every corner of
the Prime Material, going on a literal goose hunt to find it and
make sure that it couldn't act against them. However it was
never possible to catch it or defeat it, as the Ur-goose is too
unpredictable. Additionally, some deities of mischief took
sympathy into their prey and started sabotaging in secret the
efforts to find it.
With the eons passing, deities abandoned their wild goose
chase and found a way to come to terms with the fact that the
Primordial of Havonk will always be out there somewhere,
potentially throwing off their plans and messing things up.
Some sages have been proposing that this might be one of
the (main) reasons why many deities left the Prime Material
in favor of the Outer Planes.
Monstrous Mother Goose
Monstrous Mother Goose
One of the favorite occupations of the Ur-goose is to design (if
you can call it so) and give birth to creatures made in its
image and resemblance, to make sure that disorder can be
spread in each and every corner of the multiverse.
Sometimes this is done in plain despise of the products of
other creator entities, as a form of mocking. Many are its
creations and they come in all shapes and sizes, as the
terrifying honkdra, the insidious honkatrice or the dreadful
honkraken.
All of the progenies of the Primordial of Havonk have one
thing in common: they are designed to be agents of disorder.
Some bring chaos through mischief, and others through
literal destruction. Either way, the world soon learned to
never trust a goose.
Enemy of Civilization
Enemy of Civilization
Even if the Primordial of Havonk is neither a deity or a
leader, there are organizations and individuals believing in
what it represents and embodies. Several druidic circles
worship the Ur-goose and consider it their protective spirit.
Usually, those are the ones actively working to fight back
civilization, and so they look up to what they consider the
incarnation of the most chaotic aspects of nature. By
worshiping the Ur-goose, these druidic circles aim to be
inspired for new and creative ways to disrupt the
advancements of organized society, and to ensure that it
doesn't threaten the purity of the wild world.
Gifts of Havonk
Gifts of Havonk
Here are some examples of the supernatural gifts that the Ur-
goose could deliver to a worthy bringer of havonk, and a
recommended level for each one of them (see Chapter 7 of
the Dungeon Master's Guide for rules about Supernatural
Gifts):
Charm of the Honkeous Laughter (Level 1-4). This
charm allows you to cast the honkeous laughter* spell as an
action, no components required. Once used three times, the
charm goes away.
Charm of the Wild Goose Chase (Level 5-10). One pair
of boots or shoes in your possession becomes a pair of wild
goose chasers* (DM's choice) for the next 10 days. The
charm then vanishes from you and the items go back to
normal.
Charm of the Honktropy (Level 11-16). This charm
allows you to cast the prismatic spray* spell as an action, no
components required. Once used three times, the charm goes
away.
Charm of the Primordial Havonk (Level 17-20). You own
a goose figurine made of the same materials that the Ur-
goose is made of, that you can break to activate the call of the
Ur-goose* spell. Once you do so, the figurine loses its power.

### Barbarian: Path of


### Barbarian: Path of


### Fowlish Fury


### Fowlish Fury

Barbarians who follow the Path of Fowlish Fury are fearsome
fighters that draw their strength from the chaotic energy of
the goose, and are known for their wild, erratic combat style
and unwavering aggression, often terrifying and disorienting
their opponents with vicious strikes, honks and hissing.
These barbarians possess a strong sense of loyalty and
protectiveness towards their allies, much like the way geese
defend their young.
Honking Rage
Honking Rage
3rd-level Path of the Fowlish Fury feature
When you enter a rage you can emit a loud battle honk,
audible out to a range of 60 feet, which staggers your
opponents and grants you and your allies an edge in combat.
Creatures of your choice that can see you and hear the honk
must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC equal to 8 +
your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be
frightened of you until the end of your next turn. Additionally,

### Fowlish Curses


### Fowlish Curses

The Geese of Power and their creatures are whimsical and
vengeful beings, often easy to anger. To make things worse,
they usually have a chaotic sense of humor that often gets
mixed up with their sense of justice (when they have any).
The following curses can be laid as a trap, a punishment or
a consequence of seriously crossing one of the Geese of
Power. They are intended more as a way to mess up with the
characters, than as a critical danger. Spells at the characters'
disposal might relieve these curses' effects temporarily but
can't lift them completely.
A remove curse spell cast on the victim of such a curse
suppresses the burden for 1 hour. A greater restoration spell
suppresses the burden until the victim finishes a long rest.
Death usually ends a curse, but the curse returns in full force
if the cursed character returns to life without resolving the
curse. If you want to read more information about this kind of
curse, see Van Richten's Guide to Ravenloft.
Curse of the Golden Feathers
Curse of the Golden Feathers
This is similar to the curse that falls on anybody that touches
a goose of the golden feathers, punishing the greed of
whoever wants to pick the goose's golden feathers.
Pronouncement. When Mother Goose recounts the fable
of the goose of the golden feathers, she says "Who wishes to
pluck a feather of the golden goose, will find the feather to be
as sticky as its desires."
Burden. The target that touched the protected item or
creature becomes restrained. While restrained, the target is
stuck to the item or creature and will follow it wherever it
goes, unless one of them is teleported away. Another creature
touching or hitting with a melee attack the cursed target is
also targeted by this curse, becoming stuck to the touched
creature instead.
Resolution. The curse lasts until the cursed target makes
a gesture of genuine generosity, like giving away its precious
belongings at least as worth as the average value of a magic
item for the character's level or the creature's CR (see Magic
Items in the Dungeon Master's Guide). Separating with
teleportation the target and the touched object or creature
also breaks the curse.
Feathered Doom
Feathered Doom
One of Anserath's favorite tricks, it turns the creature into an
archenemy of all goosekind.
Pronouncement. The creature hears a threatening,
otherworldly honk that is full of hate.
Burden. Every goose creature with Intelligence 3 or lower
is hostile to the cursed creature and attacks it on sight. Other
goose creatures make a DC 12 Wisdom saving, becoming
hostile to the cursed creature on a failed save.
Resolution. The target must become part of a flock of
goose creatures for a full, uninterrupted week. If the target
approaches the flock with this intention in mind, then a DC
20 Wisdom (Animal Handling) check is enough to prevent the
geese from attacking it (unless the geese have other reasons
to attack). During the week, the target must spend at least 8
hours per day in the company of its flock, behaving just like
them. After doing so, the curse is lifted.
Killing the Golden Goose
Killing the Golden Goose
Killing a creature protected by this curse is a sure way to lose
one's wealth.
Pronouncement. The fabled goose of the golden eggs
provides one golden egg per day. Who kills it, usually in hope
to harvest more golden eggs from the goose's dead body,
brings down this curse on itself. From this comes the idiom
"killing the goose of the golden eggs", known to many.
Burden. The target has disadvantage on any ability check
to find precious goods or accumulate wealth of any kind.
Whenever one or more dice are rolled to randomly determine
the amount or the value of treasures or items found by the
character, the result is always the one less convenient for the
target (like the lowest amount of coins, or the highest price
for a magic item).
Resolution. The protected creature that was killed must
be brought back to life, or the target must find an egg from a
golden goose, hatch it, protect it and nurture it personally
until it becomes adult. This usually takes six months.

Uncanny Goose
Uncanny Goose
The creature crossed the geese of power and now is being
stalked by a magical, unkillable goose.
Pronouncement. This curse is usually signaled by a goose-
shaped birthmark on the body of the target. Depending on
the effect intended by the cursing power, killing such a
creature can transmit the curse to the killer.
Burden. Whenever the target rolls a 1 on the d20 for an
ability check, attack roll or saving throw, a domestic goose
uncannily appears in a free space within 20 feet of the target.
The goose is immune to all divination and mind-reading
magical effects, and any kind of magic effectively registers
that the goose is not there (but it is). The goose can be killed
or imprisoned, but comes back to life at full hit points or frees
itself as soon as nobody is watching it.
Resolution. The target must be transformed into a beast
that is a goose creature, then receive the remove curse spell
or similar magic.
Webbed feet
Webbed feet
A territory that is sacred to geese will curse the feet of who
walks through it.
Pronouncement. By the entrance of the area, a writing in
Abyssal, Celestial or Primordial says: "Who dares to step in
the land of geese, will only take goose steps."
Burden. The targets receives the legs effect of the web
limbs* spell.
Resolution. The target has to befriend a goose creature.
Woeful Honking
Woeful Honking
The character seriously disrespected a goose creature or
goosekind in general, and will now suffer the consequences
by sounding just like the thing that the character offended.
Pronouncement. The curse can be pronounced by a
powerful goose creature or spellcaster, or just be part of the
folklore and rituals revolving around geese. The curse usually
recites: "Whoever will speak ill of the goose, will then speak
just as the goose".
Burden. When the target first receives the curse, and when
the target rolls a 1 on the d20 for an ability check, attack roll
or saving throw, it can express itself only through honking for
10 minutes (therefore can't cast spells with verbal
components or use magic items that require an activation
word). Roll a d20 every 10 minutes: on a result from 11 to 20,
the target's speech ability goes back to normal.
Resolution. The target must create, commission or find a
work of art that shows the beauty, power or good qualities of
geese, and donate it to a place dedicated to them (like a
temple of the Great Golden Goose.)
Wrath of the Goose
Wrath of the Goose
The mind of the character is shattered by fowlish thoughts,
becoming confused and dangerous. This curse usually
originates from Anserath, the Honking Chaos.
Pronouncement. By the protected area, object or creature,
it is possible to hear several chaotic honks that come from
nowhere but can be heard directly in the mind.
Burden. When the target rolls a 1 on the d20 for an ability
check, attack roll or saving throw, it receives the effects of the
honkeous laughter* spell. If this happens again while the
target is under the effect of this spell, the target uses its
reaction to move towards the closest non-goose creature and
make a melee attack against it.
Resolution. The target must find the transcendent peace
of the Great Golden Goose. This can be achieved by going
through a migration of enlightenment (a quest at the DM's
choice), touching the Golden Egg of Divinity, or receiving a
remove curse spell from a goose creature that considers the
target worthy of it.

Golden Egg of Divinity
Golden Egg of Divinity
Wondrous Item, Artifact (Requires Attunement)
The Golden Egg of Divinity is an object of legend, about
which famous bards have written tales, many of which are
particularly loved by children. Some say it was laid by the
Great Golden Goose itself, others say it's the same egg from
which the Great Golden Goose was generated. All this does
nothing but fuel the age-old question, which came first: the
goose or the egg? However, what all the stories agree on is
that this palm-sized golden egg has a perfectly smooth and
totally undamaged surface. It effuses a pleasant warmth to
the touch, and despite its delicate appearance it seems totally
indifferent to any impact or damage. Throughout history, only
a lucky few have come into possession of the Egg, which has
always brought them great prosperity. It is equally well
known that when feelings of greed and evil have made their
way into the heart of those who possessed the Egg, it has
always brought great misfortune before mysteriously
disappearing.
Benevolent Magic Focus. The Golden Egg of Divinity can
be used as a spellcasting focus by any spellcaster attuned to
it, while carried or held. An evil creature that attunes to the
Golden Egg of Divinity immediately suffers the effects of the
Curse of the Golden Feather (see "Curses" earlier in this
chapter) that can't be prevented in any way.
Random Properties. The Golden Egg of Divinity has the
following randomly determined properties:
2 minor beneficial properties
2 major beneficial properties
Spells. The Golden Egg of Divinity has 10 charges and
regains 1d6 + 4 expended charges daily at dawn. While
carrying or holding it you can use an action and expend 1 or
more charges to cast one of the following spells (save DC 18
and +4 spellcasting ability modifier, or your spell save DC and
your spellcasting ability modifier, if higher) among: calm
emotion (2 charges), cure wounds (1 charge per spell level, up
to 5th), fabricate (4 charges), gaze of the golden goose* (6
charges), goodberry (1 charge), greater restoration (5

curse (3 charges). You can also use an action to cast one of

using any charge: animal friendship, detect evil and good,

action required) whenever you or any other creature within a
60-foot radius of the Egg makes an attack roll, an ability
check, or a saving throw, to roll an additional d20 and use the
one that uses the highest number. You can choose to do this
after the roll, but before the outcome is determined. When
you do this no other creature, even with the Lucky feat, can

outcome of the roll any further.
Prosperity. Daily at dawn the Egg can create one

its owner repeatedly behaves in a greedy, selfish or cruel way.

however there is a 10% chance that this is still not enough to
stop it.
Destroying the Golden Egg of Divinity. It is said that the
Golden Egg of Divinity cannot be destroyed until time itself is
destroyed and the question “which came first” is answered.
However, cooking the Golden Egg for 24 hours in the heat of
the Elemental Plane of Fire and then feeding it to the
tarrasque makes it disappear for 1d100 years.

The Honkonomicon is the realization of an absurd ambition:
to control chaos through chaos. The book’s cover was bound
in the skin of one of the many heads of Anserath, its pages
are made of paper fabricated by the Ur-goose, and they were

whole crafting process happened in the Limbo, the plane of
pure chaos, where the mind has power over matter and
energy. By doing so, they created a book that could hold all
knowledge (both magical and mundane) about goosekind,
and a tool to realize the impossible: to control the geese.
However, it had to be an imperfect tool, as the magic of the
Honkonomicon (and the geese it must control) is chaotic by
default. Even if carrying the book gives great power over
goosekind, using it can always carry with it some
unpredictable consequence. After its creation, the
Honkonomicon passed into uncountable different hands,
with many expert (and ambitious) spellcasters finding their

### Tag)

For the purpose of this book, a "goose creature" is a creature
with the Goose tag. Several components of this book refer to
goose creatures, like the spell conjure honkland beings or the
Honkonomicon artifact. You will see that all of the creatures

The DM could expand the Goose tag to creatures from
other books when it makes sense to do so, such as for the
monster from Circle of the Honk.

### Anserath


### Anserath

Anserath, the Honking Chaos, is the demon lord of anger and
pointless destruction. Originally created by Pazuzu as his war
dog, Anserath immediately fell out of control and ran away
from its master. After doing so, the Honking Chaos first
joined Demogorgon, then abandoned him too to go on its
own way. Now Anserath nests in the Seeping Woods of
Yeenoghu's Realm, claiming no allegiance to any demon other
than itself and frequently scouring through the Abyss in
search for an excuse to move battle to anybody or anything.
The demon lord is also known as the Raging Mouths
because of its insatiable desire to bite and eat anything on its
trail. The dozens of goose heads composing Anserath's body
are perpetually honking in a mixture of anger and hunger,
and will never lose the chance to try to "taste" something in
front of them. As primordial chaos flows strongly inside of
the demon lord, creatures bitten by it can experience severe
avian mutations that can eventually turn the victim into an
actual demogoose, a demonic goosish horror.
See Chapter 1 for more information about Anserath.
Anserath's Lair
Anserath's Lair
Anserath has a permanent nest in the Seeping Woods, on the
layer of the Abyss known as Yeenoghu's Realm. However, the
Honking Chaos is often wreaking havonk somewhere else
and has the habit to temporarily nest wherever it’s spending
some time. The Raging Mouth's lair is a tangle of materials
picked from its surroundings, and extremely foul.

### Demogoose


### Demogoose

One of the noisiest and filthiest spawns of the Abyss, the
demogoose is a fiend that exists as the result of Anserath's
chaotic corruption on another life-form. Since even just the
mere vicinity of the Honking Chaos is enough to make any
hatching egg generate a demogoose, these fowlish horrors
follow the demon lord's path and are considered an ominous
sign of its influence over a region.
These fire breathing fiends are considered hateful and
undesirable even by demon standards, since when a
demogoose shakes its wings to fly, it releases all kinds of filth
around itself. Also the demon adores to terrorize the enemies
with its honk and then jump on them, taking advantage of the
terror and mayhem that it just induced.
Demogeese incarnate primordial chaos to the point of
making it impossible to restrain them. This, added to the fact
that they know no fear, is particularly frustrating for cultists
or even more powerful demons that wish to control them for
their purpose. Whoever ends up summoning a demogoose
(willingly or by mistake) soon realizes that they are better
employed as a tool to wreck pure havoc, than as part of any
careful planning.
Demogoose of Many Mouth
Demogoose of Many Mouth
Being an embodiment of raw chaos, demogeese are destined
to be constantly warping and evolving (if you can call it so)
even more than other members of demonkind. A demogoose
that survives long enough and eats plenty of living creatures
can eventually become a demogoose of many mouths: a
stronger, bigger and more horrifying incarnation of
Anserath's wrath and hunger, and closer to the demon lord's
unnatural shape.
When a demogoose of many mouths arrives in an area
where other minor members of its kind are present, all
regular demogeese (and possibly some other evil goose
creatures) can start to follow it, forming a flock that is gonna
try to nest in the area. Then they will try to steal eggs from
other creatures that, when kept in the nest for long
enough, will spawn brand new demogeese. If not
destroyed in time, these fiendish nests can soon go out of
control and become a very big, honking concern for
everyone in the region.

### Great Golden Goose


### Great Golden Goose

The timeless Great Golden Goose is widely considered the
godmother of all goosekind and a symbol of freedom,
prosperity and transcending enlightenment. While the
Mother of Gold is a being of light and virtue, she's not to be
mistaken as a passive entity, as both her and her children (the
celestial geese of the golden feathers) are famous for
bestowing curses on whoever might deserve them.
An easy way to anger the Great Golden Goose is to abuse
the gift delivered by her, act with excessive greed or hurt her
"flock" or sacred temples. At the same time, she also
embodies some of the most temperamental aspects of
goosedom, making it easy to trigger her anger even with
minor infractions. It is not unknown for a high rank cultist of
the Great Golden Goose to go on a quest to find its patron, in
the purpose to appease her rage (or at least understand the
reason for it) after mysterious curses are being laid for
apparently no reason.
See Chapter 1 for more information about the lore, cults
and powers of the Great Golden Goose.
The Age-old Question
The Age-old Question
Who came first, the egg or the goose? Not even the wisest
sages know the answer. As much as nobody understands
when and where the Great Golden Goose came to be, also
none can say if she will ever find her end. When (and if) the
Great Golden Goose dies or is destroyed, it comes back to life
after one year and one day, hatching from one of the several
golden eggs that exist in the world. For the first eighty-eight
days of her life, she's indistinguishable from a goose of the
golden feathers* and has its same statistics. Then, suddenly,
it will evolve into her final, divine incarnation of avian glory
once more, while rewarding or punishing whoever treated
her particularly kindly or cruelly in the meanwhile.
The only ways to stop the rejuvenation of the Great Golden
Goose is to rid the multiverse of any golden egg (an almost
impossible task) or to prevent her spirit from traveling freely
after death.
The Great Golden Gooses's Lair
The Great Golden Gooses's Lair
The Great Golden Goose follows a perpetual migration that
begins in the Feywild, travels through each of the good-
aligned planes (Ysgard, Arborea, the Beastlands, Elysium,
Bytopia and Mount Celestia) and ends in in the Astral Sea.
The migration is a metaphorical journey from passion to
asceticism, starting in the planes that are more connected to
strong emotions, and finishing in the ones that embody
elevation and transcendence. The Goose has a nest in each of
these planes, always in a location that's particularly remote
and dangerous to reach.

### Ur-goose


### Ur-goose

The Ur-goose, also known as the Primordial Havonk, is an
ancestral being who embodies natural chaos in its raw state,
considered to be the first goose that ever walked on earth
(even if the timeless Great Golden Goose might have
preceded it) and gave birth to several exemplars of goosekind.
The Ur-goose's body is a towering 40-foot tall mass of raw
elements that are perpetually swirling, shifting and mixing
with each other, as they can never find a stable form. At the
core of the Ur-goose lies a brilliant nebula, which is the
source of its creative power.
Despite its immense size, the titan's shifting body is
capable of concealing itself with great ease, usually by mixing
with the elemental matter of its surrounding environment. In
fact, the Primordial Havonk is renowned to be one the most
clever tricksters and escapists of the multiverse. After
defeating the primordials at the dawn of time, the gods have
been hunting for the Ur-goose incessantly for eons, with no
success. In fact, the Primordial Havonk takes great joy into
seeing the frustration of other beings going on a wild goose
chase to find it or catch it, often with poor or no results.
Even if the Ur-goose is often running away or hiding, there
are times when it might decide to take a stance and fight
back, or even to actively move war to its enemies. This is
usually the case when civilization is trying to impose its
"order" on an untainted natural area, or when something
poses a great danger to goosekind as whole. When the Ur-
goose is fighting the rules of matter, magic and reality
constantly bend around its chaotic essence, while its honk

### Weregoose

A weregoose is an absolute agent of chaos. As humanoids,
they develop longer necks, smaller eyes and thicker lips. In
their humanoid forms, they prefer weapons that enable them
to create the most mayhem in the shortest amount of time,
like greatclubs and flails, but they are clever enough to
change to a more suitable weapon if they need more finesse.
Weregeese infect other humanoids out of spite, to punish
someone that misbehaved or just to create more chaos.
Sometimes, they might infect somebody that they really wish
to protect.
When they can, weregeese gather in very tight
communities, developing strong interpersonal bonds and a
great desire to protect the weakest members.

### Adventure Hoonks

First Tier (Level 1-4)
First Tier (Level 1-4)
Night of the Goosish Deads
Night of the Goosish Deads
Lately, a local farmer has been noticing more and more of his
geese disappearing. Yesterday night, the farmer found that
several of his geese are back, but they are now goose
skeletons* that attack him or anything else (including the
characters) on sight. Even so, the skeletons are friendly
towards the geese of their own former flock and they act just
like regular geese, as if they never left the flock.
Following the tracks of the skeletons leads the party to the
hideout of a necromancer, that planned to raise a flock of
undead geese. Things went wrong when the necromancer
successfully summoned a demogoose* but lost control of it,
leading to the necromancer's death. The hideout is still full of
goose skeletons*, goose zombies* and the dreaded
demogoose* itself.
When Things Go Swine
When Things Go Swine
A local aristocrat lady woke up to realize that her daughter,
who was supposed to get married the day after, is missing.
Instead, there is a violent boar in her bedroom, still dressed
as the daughter, and a small wooden flask with a label saying
"Goose Wine" on the floor. The boar is actually the betrothed
daughter, that drank a cursed goose wine* potion created to
turn the victim into a boar indefinitely. The daughter drank it
as part of a bet with a foreigner, that was actually a druid
devoted to the Ur-goose.
The druid transformed the daughter as part of a plan go get
revenge against the mother, who has plans to expand the city
at the expense of the surrounding woods and swamps. The
druid is accompanied by a giant goose, and has the
obedience of a flock of geese that faithfully guards the
wilderness.
The druid always carries the antidote to reverse the
transformation. The characters could solve this in many
ways, like taking the antidote by force, stealth, or trying to
strike a deal between the druid and the lady.
Second Tier (Level 5-10)
Second Tier (Level 5-10)
Gaze of the Megoosa
Gaze of the Megoosa
The goose of the golden feathers from the local temple of
the Great Golden Goose, which was worshiped as a living
saint, recently disappeared in a mysterious battle that killed
half of the people in the temple. At the same time, people
have been disappearing in the city, and after they do, a
domestic goose* eventually goes to their house in their
place.
After some research, the characters can discover that this
is the doing of a megoosa* that recently established her lair
nearby the city. The cursed monstrosity also has a
honkimera* doing her bidding, which she deployed to kidnap
the goose of the golden feathers: the megoosa knows that the
power of the golden goose can revert her transformations,
but doesn't dare to kill it because is too afraid of being cursed
again.
If the megoosa is not stopped, she will keep transforming
everyone in the city and province, until it becomes populated
by people just as cursed as she is.
The Child of Mother Goose
The Child of Mother Goose
The director of a local orphanage, a sweet granny known as
Mother Goose, recently disappeared after visiting one of her
"children" (an orphan that grew up and left her institution a
long time ago). Without her, the orphanage will lose its only
patron and might eventually be forced to close.
Mother Goose is being forcefully held by a gray honker*
that she nurtured many years ago. The honker, afraid to loose
her granny again, refuses to let her go. Even if Mother Goose
is actually a strong magical being in disguise, the gray honker
is now immune to her magical influence after being exposed
to it for its whole childhood.
Mother Goose wishes to be freed, and will fight alongside
the characters (aiding them with her magic) as long as they
don't try to kill "her child". She will reward the characters
with a cane of the silly goose* and other treasures if they
manage to free her without killing the honker.
If the characters fail or abandon the quest, the gray honker
will eventually kill Mother Goose by accident, then go into a
crazy rampage and lay waste to the whole region.
Third Tier (Level 11-16)
Third Tier (Level 11-16)
On the Wings of the Rhonk
On the Wings of the Rhonk
A rhonk* has been sighted in the region recently. Shortly
after, the immense monstrosity started wreaking havoc by
several major cities. When the characters approach it, they
will notice that the rhonk is wearing saddle, harnesses and
emblems, all proofs that the creature actually belongs to a
cloud giant lord.
Truth is, the rhonk escaped from its master's leash. If the
characters kill the rhonk, they will anger the giant lord that
will go out of his way to hurt the characters and make their
lives impossible. On the other hand, if the characters find a
way to tame or capture the creature, its master will greatly
compensate them. Once tamed, the rhonk could even bring
the characters back to its owner.
The giant lord lives in a castle in the clouds, high in the sky,
and is obsessed with geese. He owns a goose of the golden
feathers* that can lay golden eggs, as well as several magic
items related to geese. He's also looking for a copy of the
Honkonomicon artifact.

Fourth Tier (Level 17-20)
The Lost Egg
The Lost Egg
On a big island in the middle of the ocean lies the great
Kingdhonk of Goonitch, where geese and humanoids lived in
perfect harmony... Until now. Recently, the Golden Egg of
Divinity has been stolen from the local Cathedral of the Great
Golden Goose, and from that moment the peace between
humanoids and goosekind was broken. The theft angered the
Mother of Gold, who is now laying her curses on the
Kingdhonk and driving several goose creatures crazy. Now,
chaos reigns everywhere.
The Egg was actually stolen by a honkraken, but was
bribed by the king himself to do so: he desires the Egg only
for himself.
