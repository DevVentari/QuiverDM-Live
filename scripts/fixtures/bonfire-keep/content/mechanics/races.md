# Skaven

*The underways have always been there. Beneath Aurelios, beneath the Covenant's port cities, beneath the trading posts that line the Great Roads — below the foundations of everything the surface world built and called civilization — there are tunnels. Not the Empire's infrastructure tunnels, not the sewers, not the catacombs that polite histories mention and impolite ones explain. Tunnels older than most of those structures. Tunnels that connect things that were never meant to be connected, that know where the walls are thin, that were built by something the surface world would rather not acknowledge lives beneath it.*

*The Skaven have been down there since before the Binding. They will be down there long after.*

---

Skaven are a people the surface world tends to discuss in terms of what they do — steal, plot, swarm, scheme — rather than what they are, which is a civilization of considerable sophistication that has made a studied practice of being underestimated. In the warlord-dominated warrens and the seer-controlled research burrows and the ancient institutional structures that a surface scholar would recognize as a functioning society if they could get past their immediate reaction to the smell, the Skaven have been conducting their own version of history for centuries without consulting anyone on the surface about any of it.

Some Skaven make their way up. Exiles, operatives, individuals whose ambitions or errors or rare moments of something like conscience have placed them on the wrong side of whatever the current power structure considers acceptable. They arrive in the surface world with specific skills — extraordinary speed, a relationship with disease that reads as either horrifying or useful depending on your immediate needs, and the particular insight of someone who has spent their life reading rooms where the wrong answer gets you eaten.

**Clan Mors** is the shadow that follows them. The old assassination clan controls significant portions of the underway infrastructure beneath Hameria Ire's major cities, and its relationship with its graduates is the specific kind of ownership that does not recognize resignation. A Skaven on the surface without Clan Mors's blessing is, from the clan's perspective, an escaped asset. The surface world's politics are complicated enough. Add a long arm reaching up from below, and the complications compound.

## Skaven Traits

Your Skaven character has the following racial traits.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Age.** Skaven mature rapidly, reaching adulthood by age 5 and rarely living past 40 due to violence or disease.

**Alignment.** Most Skaven are chaotic and selfish, with a tendency toward evil. However, some rare individuals break free from their society's cruelty.

**Size.** Skaven are slightly shorter than humans, standing between 4 and 5 feet tall, but are wiry and quick. Your size is Medium.

**Speed.** Your base walking speed is 35 feet. You have a climb speed of 15 feet and a swim speed of 20 feet.

**Darkvision.** Accustomed to living underground, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light and darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

**Keen Smell.** You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell.

**Plagueborn.** You have resistance to poison damage and advantage on saving throws against disease.

**Cowardly Nature.** You have disadvantage on saving throws against being frightened.

**Vermin Agility.** When you take the Disengage action, you can move an additional 10 feet as part of that action.

**Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Undercommon.

## Skaven Subraces

The Skaven warrens are not monolithic. Different clans specialize in different capacities — the teeming masses of the clanrat armies, the plague-carrier specialists whose bodies have become arguments, the warlocks who work the warp-energies the surface world's mages have no framework for. Choose one of the following subraces.

### Clanrat

The backbone of Skaven society and the surface world's most common encounter with it — fast, vicious, and far more effective in groups than their individual statistics suggest. A single clanrat is a manageable problem. The thing that makes clanrats dangerous is that they know this, and they do not arrive alone.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Constitution score increases by 1.
- **Swarm Tactics.** When you have an ally within 5 feet of a creature you are attacking, you gain a +1 bonus to your attack roll against that creature.
- **Scavenge.** You have proficiency with the Sleight of Hand skill.

### Plague Skaven

Clan Mors's most specific and most terrible innovation: an operative whose presence is the weapon. The Plague Skaven are what emerge from the clan's research into living disease — individuals whose bodies have been modified, through processes the clan's training manuals describe as graduation and the graduates describe in other ways, to carry and transmit afflictions that the clan can control and the target cannot. What Clan Mors discovered, and what some plague Skaven on the surface have discovered in turn, is that the modification is a tool — and tools belong to whoever holds them.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Wisdom score increases by 1.
- **Plague Aura.** As a bonus action, you can emit a wave of sickness. Creatures of your choice within 5 feet of you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be poisoned until the end of their next turn. You can use this ability a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Disease Lore.** You have proficiency in the Medicine skill.

### Warlock Skaven

The engineers. The researchers. The ones who looked at the warp-energy that threads through the underways and asked not "what does this do to the body" but "what can I make it do." Warlock Skaven on the surface tend to be either extremely useful or extremely dangerous, often simultaneously, and they have a way of finding magical phenomena that the surface world's established traditions haven't catalogued yet — because they were looking at a completely different set of source materials.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Your Intelligence score increases by 1.
- **Tinkerer's Instinct.** You gain proficiency with tinker's tools.
- **Warp Sense.** You can cast the *detect magic* spell once per long rest without expending a spell slot, using Intelligence as your spellcasting ability for this spell.

## Names

Skaven names are harsh and sibilant, often reflecting their clan affiliations or personal aspirations.

**Male Names:** Skreek, Snikt, Snikch, Skrit, Skreekal, Tretch, Ikit, Queek  
**Female Names:** Skritta, Snikla, Skreela, Trikket, Snaka, Vreeka  
**Clan Names:** Slinktail, Quickfang, Plagetooth, Rustfur, Warpclaw, Pestilent, Steelfang

## Skaven in Hameria Ire

A Skaven on the surface of Hameria Ire is always at the intersection of two worlds — the one they left behind and the one they have arrived in — and the assumption of most people in the second world is that they know which side of that intersection the Skaven is serving.

The assumption is not always wrong.

What makes a surface Skaven's situation genuinely complicated is that Hameria Ire is in crisis, and crisis is the condition under which the surface world's usual categories break down fastest. The three great powers are managing a wound they cannot acknowledge, and the cracks in their management are exactly the places where someone with the underway's perspective sees things the factions above cannot afford to see. The surface world is not short of players. It is short of people who were not raised inside the particular stories the surface world tells about itself.

A Skaven who came up from below and stayed — who chose the complicated surface over the brutal familiar — has already made one impossible decision. They are, by that decision, exactly the kind of person the Heartflame tends to recognize.

What they do with that recognition is the question that follows them.
