# The Pregenitor Orb — Serenitas's Proof

*Before she broke herself apart, before the world had taken its first breath, she built one last thing.*

Not a weapon. Not a throne. A vessel — small enough to hold in two hands, cool and dark and patient, etched with symbols in a language that predates language. She poured into it the shape of a question, and then she scattered it across the world she was about to breathe into being, and she waited to see if anyone would ever understand what they were being asked.

The **Pregenitor Orb** is not an artifact of power, though it will become one. It is an artifact of *comprehension*. Serenitas designed it to be completed only when mortals had demonstrated genuine understanding of each of the five forces she embodied — not by studying them, not by channeling them through prayer, but by *living through them* in ways that leave a mark.

The Orb exists in five **Fragments**, scattered across Hameria Ire, each dormant until the person carrying it has passed through the experience its Aspect demands. They cannot be unlocked by force. They cannot be stolen into activation. A tyrant who seized the Fragment of Justice would find it cold and dark in their fist, because justice understood only as power is not justice at all.

That is the test.

---

## The Five Fragments

### The Fragment of Command
*The Aspect of Justice — Trial of the Arbiter, carried now by Ambric*

What is asked: *Can you hold authority without becoming what you feared?*

The Fragment of Command does not ask for obedience. It asks for the harder thing — the understanding that order without compassion is simply control with better manners. The soul who carries this fragment will be put in the position of power over others at precisely the moment when abusing that power would be easiest and most justifiable. The Fragment watches. It waits for the choice.

When the debt is paid, the Fragment warms.

### The Fragment of Growth
*The Aspect of Thymal — Trial of the Great Root*

What is asked: *Can you tend something that is not yours to own?*

Growth is patient in ways that mortals rarely are. The Fragment of Growth seeks people who have learned to invest in outcomes they will not live to see — to plant without harvesting, to teach without controlling, to protect without possessing. It is the Aspect that most confounds the Empire, who believe that what flourishes must be managed. Thymal always disagreed.

When the debt is paid, the Fragment quickens.

### The Fragment of Adaptation
*The Aspect of Orym — Trial of the First Mariner*

What is asked: *Can you change without losing what you were?*

The Tidal Covenant understands this better than anyone. Adaptation without identity is just dissolution — becoming whatever the current demands, until there is nothing left of the original. The Fragment of Adaptation is searching for the harder synthesis: people who can be reshaped by the world and still, at the end of it, know their own name.

When the debt is paid, the Fragment shifts.

### The Fragment of Memory
*The Aspect of Memory — Trial of the First Chronicler, witnessed by Faeren*

What is asked: *Can you carry a truth that costs you something to hold?*

Memory without courage is just nostalgia. The Fragment of Memory does not reward people who remember fondly. It rewards people who remember *honestly* — who hold the uncomfortable histories, the crimes of people they love, the versions of events that make their own side look worse. In a world where the Empire has spent fourteen centuries rewriting its own record, an honest memory is a radical act.

When the debt is paid, the Fragment speaks.

### The Fragment of Hope
*The Aspect of Serenitas herself — The Final Proof*

What is asked: *Can you accept an ending without being broken by it?*

This is the one she kept closest. The others she scattered across the world, trusting the world to bring worthy hands to them in time. This one she left in the Bonfire Keep — the place that exists between the world's breaths — because she understood that the final proof could only be given by people who had already passed through everything else.

Hope, as Serenitas understood it, is not optimism. Optimism is comfortable. Hope is the thing that survives after comfort is gone — the choice to continue tending something fragile in a world that has given you every reason to stop. She had it once, before the Salting. She is not sure she has it still.

She is watching the Keep's fire to find out if anyone else does.

When the debt is paid, the Orb becomes whole.

## The Pregenitor Artifacts — Personal Relics

Alongside the five Fragments, Serenitas left something more intimate scattered across the world: **Pregenitor Artifacts** — objects that resonate with a specific soul's relationship to one of the Aspects. Unlike the Fragments, these are not tests to be passed. They are mirrors. They reflect back to their bearer the shape of what they are becoming.

They grow as the bearer grows. They do not evolve on a schedule. They evolve when something true happens.

---

### The Warpstone Shard
*Bearer: Skreek Swicschnout — Aspect of Adaptation*

A jagged piece of cosmic forge-stone, found in a wound that shouldn't have healed. It resonates with the corruption Skreek carries in his arm — not to purify it, but to ask a question of it: *what if the thing you were told is your weakness is the thing the world needs?*

The Shard does not treat Skreek's plague arm as a sickness to be managed. It treats it as a language being learned.

| Stage | Condition | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| **Dormant** | Levels 1–4 | Resistance to Poison and Necrotic damage. +2 to attack rolls with finesse weapons. |
| **Awakened** | After the Fragment of Growth — when Skreek has invested in something he cannot control | The Shard pulses green. Bonus action: *Overgrow* the blade — 2d6 poison damage, reach extends to 10ft, lasts 1 minute. |
| **Exalted** | After the Fragment of Adaptation — when Skreek knows what he is, underneath the arm | *Phase* through reality. Teleport speed of 30ft. Attacks ignore all resistances. |

---

### The Scavenger's Practical Vest
*Bearer: Norm Alfella — Aspect of Creation*

A vest woven, somehow, from things that were discarded — fibres of unmade possibility, the material remnant of choices that were never taken. It fits Norm in the specific way that things fit people who were not supposed to have them. It is the Aspect of Creation's sense of humour: the most powerful artifact of making, given to the person who understands that the best things are usually found, not forged.

| Stage | Condition | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| **Dormant** | Levels 1–4 | 50lbs of weightless storage. Advantage on Investigation and Scavenging checks. |
| **Awakened** | After the Fragment of Memory — when Norm carries something he would rather forget | The vest *remembers*. Once per day: pull a non-magical item (100gp or less, previously seen) from a pocket that was empty. |
| **Exalted** | After the Fragment of Hope — when Norm chooses to rebuild something instead of walking away | The vest becomes a beacon. Allies within 30ft gain +5 to saves against Charm and Fright. *Fabricate* at will using scavenged materials. |

---

### The Fragment of Obsidian Mirror
*Bearer: Oriyen Vale — Aspect of Justice*

A shard of the mirror the First Chronicler used to watch the world — Ambric's mirror, before he descended into mortality and traded the long view for the muddy one. It reflects not faces but *weight*: the accumulated consequence of choices, visible only to the one who holds it and is honest enough to look.

It has never shown anyone a flattering image. It has, occasionally, shown people true ones.

| Stage | Condition | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| **Dormant** | Levels 1–4 | Once per day: see the consequence of a recent decision. +2 to attack rolls with monk weapons. |
| **Awakened** | After the Fragment of Command — when Oriyen holds power and uses it justly at cost to themselves | The mirror projects consequence outward. Action: force a target to see their own weight. DC 16 Wisdom save or Paralyzed with recognition for 1 round. |
| **Exalted** | After the Fragment of Memory — when Oriyen carries a history that is not theirs but matters | The mirror becomes the **Blade of the Chronicle**. Gains Ambric's *Marked Name* property: Oriyen perceives who is fated in an encounter and whether they are owed rescue, aid, or reckoning. |

## The Final Choice — What Completing the Orb Actually Means

When all five Fragments are gathered and the Orb is whole, the party will stand at the center of the void before Serenitas herself — not the Corrupted Shepherd, not the monster that fourteen centuries of imprisonment have made of her, but the First Mother underneath, the one who built the test and has been waiting since before the world had a name to see if anyone could pass it.

The Orb does not grant power over the Grand Harvest. It grants *standing* — the right to speak to her as something that has earned the conversation.

What the party does with that conversation is the whole of the campaign.

The artifacts can be sacrificed to fuel the Orb's completion — offered back to Serenitas as proof that what they represent was truly understood, not merely wielded. Or they can be kept, and carried into whatever world comes after, as the tools of mortals who have chosen to tend it themselves.

She will accept either answer. She built the test because she genuinely did not know which one she hoped for.

*She still doesn't.*
