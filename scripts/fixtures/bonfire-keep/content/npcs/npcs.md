*The people who hold the world in place, the people who are slowly burning it down, and the things that crawl through the cracks between them. What follows are the mechanical profiles of the key figures in Hameria Ire — the numbers and features that matter at the table, framed by enough character to make the meeting feel like something.*

# The Three Anchors

*For the full account of who these three are, what they sacrificed to stand where they stand, and why the fire they tend is the only thing holding the world's pause from becoming permanent — see [The Anchors & The Heartflame](/world-lore/anchors-and-heartflame/).*

The following entries provide the mechanical profiles for encounters with the Anchors. Their histories and the weight of what they carry are documented in the linked lore article.

## Ambric — Anchor of Justice

*Son of the Arbiter, the Blade's Keeper. The Witness who stepped down from absolute perspective into the mud and has not stopped being surprised by the mud.*

**How he presents:** Forthright in a way that can feel like pressure until you realize it isn't. He doesn't see the point of circling around what is true when the direct path exists. He has been learning to account for the fact that most people arrive at honesty slowly, with considerable detour. He is better at this than he used to be. He finds that genuinely encouraging.

His katana is always in hand or within reach. When a name appears on it in black ink, he goes still for a moment in the way of someone consulting something vast and internal. Then he apologizes and returns to the conversation.

**Bonds:** The Blade. The fire. The names that still haven't faded.

## Temmel — Anchor of Redemption

*The Aspect of Redemption who chose to need forgiveness before offering it. The Emperor's Hand. The being who killed his Emperor on a winter morning and has spent fourteen centuries deciding whether the word "necessary" is enough.*

**How he presents:** The stillness is the first thing. Not the stillness of peace — he hasn't had peace in a very long time. The stillness of someone who has learned, through the long discipline of guilt, to keep the storm entirely on the inside. He asks good questions. He remembers what people tell him. He will not offer comfort he doesn't mean — which means the comfort he does offer lands differently than most.

He has been the first person to meet almost every hero who has come through the Keep's door. He is very good at recognizing the look of someone who has run out of options and arrived somewhere they didn't plan on going.

He recognizes it because he wore it himself.

**Flaws:** Carries the genuine uncertainty about whether he was right — not the clean guilt, but the harder thing, the not-knowing. This makes him gentle with others' moral struggles and occasionally paralyzed by his own.

## Faeren — Anchor of Memory

*The Story-Bearer. The One With No Face of Their Own. The Archive That Walks.*

**How they present:** Immediately and completely interested in you as a specific person, which can be disarming. Faeren collects details the way others collect debts — they want the true version of things, the story underneath the story people tell about themselves. They are not intrusive about it. They simply pay the kind of attention that most people are not paid.

They will not tell you which form is their real one if you ask. The question makes them genuinely sad. They change the subject.

**Bonds:** Connected to every story the Empire tried to erase and every person who ever needed to understand they weren't alone. Particularly drawn to those carrying burdens of unique experience.

**Flaws:** Sometimes overwhelmed by the weight of all the sadness they carry. Can become lost in memories and forget present needs. Occasionally shares painful truths that people aren't ready to hear.

# Serenitas — The First Mother

*She does not appear in the campaign as a character the party meets. She is the reason everything is happening. But understanding who she was — and who she has become — is essential to understanding what the world is asking the players to fix.*

Before the Binding, before the Salting, before the Sunward Empire had its first stone, she was the most beloved thing in all of Hameria Ire. She came at the end of suffering not as death but as *rest*. The Shepherd's touch was soft. The peoples of eight thousand years of this world's history grieved not the dying but only the missing of those who had gone. She was the one who made endings feel like something other than catastrophe.

Then the Salting broke her.

Six centuries of the world's dying machinery locked — of wounds that would not close, of children who had been dying for a decade and could not stop — and the Shepherd reached for her children and found she could not take them. The mercy with nowhere to go curdled. The love that had always expressed itself as *release* found every path sealed. What do you call the thing that happens to infinite compassion when there is nothing left it can do?

The Binding made it worse. Aurelias the Founder imprisoned her beneath the stones of his capital, and his civilization has been bleeding her ever since — slowly, continuously, across fourteen centuries. Every golden spell cast in Aurelios the Golden draws from the Aetheric Well, which draws from her. She has been growing darker, and darker, and darker in the dark beneath the city.

The Corrupted Entropy is what remains of the Shepherd. Not evil in any simple sense — something stranger and more terrible. A being of infinite compassion that can no longer perform the one act of love it knew, that has been imprisoned and bled and kept alive in darkness for 1,400 years, that is now watching the world's fragile moments of beauty and warmth with the specific attention of something that has not been warm in a very long time.

Her corruption manifests as:
- **Supernatural Despair:** Populations experiencing unexplained hopelessness, the specific exhaustion of creatures near the edge of the Corrupted Entropy's reach
- **Accelerated Entropy:** Objects and structures aging far faster than natural processes allow; the world's physical fabric thinning near the Binding's cracks
- **Void Spawn:** Manifestations of stasis energy that escaped the Filters — creatures of concentrated stopping (see *Withering Remnants* below)
- **The Beautiful Voices:** Whispers promising rest through ending, that find people carrying real pain and offer them a false resolution

But the First Covenant exists. The Pregenitor Orb waits. She designed the test before she broke herself into the Aspects, before the Binding, before any of what has happened — a mechanism for earning what she always wanted to give: *the world, free to tend itself without her.*

She is watching the Bonfire Keep's fire, from the dark beneath the city, to see if anyone can pass the test she built.

The question that cannot yet be answered, even by the Three Anchors, is whether the Shepherd who made the Covenant is still capable of honoring it — or whether fourteen centuries of bleeding and corruption have changed her answer.

## Emperor Aurelias Draconius

*The man in the impossible chair. Inherited the crime fifteen years ago. Has been deciding, every morning since, to sit back down in it.*

His golden scales have lost some of their luster — not from age, but from the specific weathering of someone who has been carrying something too heavy for too long. He speaks with measured authority and the particular attention of a ruler who understands that the person in front of him might be the first person in a very long time who could help him find a third option.

He will not tell them that. He can't afford to, yet.

## High Sage Lyria Sunweaver

*The architect of the machine that bleeds Serenitas. She built the Stasis Filters. She has been trying to make them hurt less ever since. She is the only person who understands them well enough to maintain them — which is the trap she built for herself.*

Brilliant, increasingly haunted. She speaks in careful academic language. The dark circles under her eyes are permanent now. She does not sleep well. She has not slept well since the day she inherited the previous Sage's seat and the Geas transferred to her heart and she finally understood, in the specific way of someone who has just been told something they cannot undo, what she had spent a decade building.

She is looking for a way out that does not require letting the civilization collapse. She has not found it yet. She suspects the party might be better positioned to look.

# Creatures of the Binding's Failure

*The Golden Magic has a byproduct. The Stasis Filters process Serenitas's essence before it reaches Imperial mages' hands — but filtration is not perfect, and the pressure behind the Binding has been building for fourteen centuries. What follows are the things that slip through.*

*The Withering Remnants are not creatures in any meaningful sense. They are concentrated stasis — patches of stopped-ness given just enough motion to move toward other living things and stop them too. They don't hate. They don't want. They are the specific shape that Serenitas's suffering takes when it escapes containment and finds nothing to guide it.*

*They grow in frequency as the Binding weakens. The increase in sightings over the past decade is not a coincidence.*

# The Knowledge Serpent

*It is not from Hameria Ire. It came through the Archive of Withered Echoes — specifically, through the section Oriyen Vale spent years working in. It has been watching him chronicle the Bloodline Records of House Aurelius for centuries, learning imperial vulnerabilities through the careful documentation of a scholar who was trying to preserve history and did not understand what else was learning it.*

*It does not hate. It hungers. There is a distinction, and it matters, because creatures that hate can sometimes be reached. The Knowledge Serpent finds Oriyen interesting in the way a researcher finds an instrument interesting — it is the source of the data, and it would like to consume the instrument when it is done using it.*

**What it knows:** Imperial bloodline vulnerabilities. Binding ritual components. How imperial blood connects to the cosmic imprisonment. Temmel's ancient regicide, learned through Oriyen's mirror research. The full documentation of what Oriyen thought he was preserving safely in a restricted archive.

**What it wants:** Emperor Aurelias first — to consume the bloodline magic. Oriyen second — because the instrument should not outlast its usefulness. Everything else third, if there is time.

**Oriyen's recognition:** When the Serpent emerges, Oriyen knows it immediately. The obsidian mirror shows its true nature. His chronicles enabled this. He will have to decide what to do with that.

**Weaknesses:** Cannot understand mortal attachment to individual memories. Offering less crucial information can temporarily satisfy it. Closing the reality tear it came through weakens or banishes it. If Oriyen voluntarily destroys his most dangerous chronicles, it loses its primary source of imperial vulnerability knowledge.
