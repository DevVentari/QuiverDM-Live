*The three great powers of Hameria Ire did not choose their current roles. The roles were assigned fourteen centuries ago by a crime committed before any of their living inhabitants were born, and they have been living inside those assignments ever since — the inheritors, the filters, and the timekeepers of a world whose clock was stopped and has been running wrong ever since.*

*The Sunward Empire: the civilization that cannot stop, because stopping means dying.*
*The Verdant Clans: the world's unwilling filter, absorbing fourteen centuries of what the Empire produces.*
*The Tidal Covenant: the world's slow creditor, watching the temporal debt compound in their waters.*

*None of them see the others clearly. All of them are running out of time.*

---

# The Sunward Empire

*The guardians who became the criminals. The civilization that saved itself and has been paying for it ever since.*

The Sunward Empire is the most powerful organized force in Hameria Ire — a Dragonborn-led imperial civilization of golden cities, professional armies, and a magical tradition that outperforms every other tradition in the world by a margin no one has yet been able to close. It emerged from the ruins of the Salting with a solution to the world's dying machinery, and it has been running on that solution for fourteen centuries.

The solution is also a crime. The Emperor knows it. The Twelve Witnesses know it. The Solar Priesthood does not — and the Commonality, the hundreds of millions of citizens living their lives inside the golden civilization, have never been given the chance to.

The Empire is not simply an antagonist. It is a civilization of people who were born into an arrangement they did not make, sustained by an infrastructure they depend on, defended by a faith that gives them genuine meaning. The court mages who conjure golden fire believe they are channeling the sun. The priests who heal with the Priesthood's light believe they are instruments of Solara's grace. The soldiers who guard the borders believe they are holding back the dark. None of these things are entirely false.

The question the Pregenitor Fragments are asking is whether the system that built this civilization can survive what it will take to end it cleanly.

*For the full account of Imperial theology, the secret of Golden Magic, and the truth of the Great Crime — see [The Solar Lie](/factions/solar-lie/).*

*For the twelve people who carry the full truth of the Binding — see [The Twelve Witnesses](/factions/twelve-witnesses/).*

# The Verdant Clans

*The oldest continuous civilization in Hameria Ire. The world's filter. The people carrying the weight of what the Empire has been exhaling for fourteen centuries.*

The Verdant Clans live in the Emerald Reach through a network of grove-cities grown from living wood, connected by a mycelial web called the Grove-Network that has served as their communication system, their cultural memory, and their collective consciousness for longer than the Empire has existed. They worship Thymal, the Aspect of Growth, and they understand what the Empire does not: that the Iremael is not punishment but purpose, and that a world preserved against the Dimming is not a world saved but a world suffocating.

This is why the Empire considers them heretics. Not because their faith is old — old faiths are tolerated, at a careful distance. But because they refuse to pretend the world is not dying, and the evidence is visible in every silver-leafed tree at the Reach's eastern edge.

The Silver Rot has been advancing for three centuries. The grove-network carries the Silver Static through every connected tree. The druids have silver in their veins. The Clans have been absorbing the exhaust of the Empire's Golden Magic for fourteen centuries, because living systems try to process what the wind brings them, and the wind has been carrying stasis ash southwest from Aurelios the Golden since the day the Filters first ran.

They are divided about what to do with this. The Grove-Speakers tend the Mother-Tree and keep the alliance. The Rootwardens prune what cannot be saved. The Thorns are burning Siphon-Stations.

*For the full account of Verdant philosophy, the Silver Rot, and the schism — see [The Verdant Burden](/factions/verdant-burden/).*

# The Tidal Covenant

*The faction that was not surprised by the end of the world. They had been preparing for it for eight thousand years. What surprised them was the direction it came from.*

The Tidal Covenant lives on the water — in floating cities that drift with the ocean's currents, in underwater settlements built around thermal gardens, in a culture organized entirely around the principle that everything changes and the only wisdom is learning to change with it. They follow Orym, the Aspect of Adaptation, and their theology treats the Iremael not as catastrophe but as the Great Tide: the force that clears old silt from the seabed so new reefs can form.

Of all the factions, the Covenant is closest to accepting the Grand Harvest. They have been preparing to meet it with grace for longer than the Empire has existed. And then the Empire stopped the Harvest, and the ocean began paying the temporal debt, and the northern currents of the Great Bay started thickening into Salt-Glass.

The Salted Stagnation is what happens when a civilization borrows time from the ocean and stops paying it back. Ships that enter the dense zones emerge a week late. Creatures caught at the stagnation's edge become Salt-Statues — aware, immobile, preserved at the moment of contact — and the Covenant has found them: sailors suspended in moments that happened years ago, their eyes still tracking, still waiting.

The Covenant is also divided. The Drifters keep moving. The Depth-Singers listen to the memories preserved in the dense water. The Anchors have chained their floating cities to the seabed, which is exactly as effective as it sounds.

*For the full account of Covenant philosophy, the Salted Stagnation, and the schism — see [The Tidal Adaptation](/factions/tidal-adaptation/).*

# The Wandering Markets

*The fourth quasi-faction. The ones who move between all borders and report to no crown.*

The Wandering Markets are not a nation. They have no territory, no standing army, and no official position in any of the three powers' political structures. What they have is access — to every trade route in Hameria Ire, to every major port and border crossing, to the specific kind of information that only accumulates in neutral spaces where people talk freely because they think no one important is listening.

The Markets are organized through **the Circle of Roads**: four coordinators representing four modes of travel, who meet by arrangement twice a year and govern by consensus the only thing all Market factions agree needs governing — the Continuity Compact.

| Name | Role | Background |
|------|------|------------|
| **Caravelle Stormwind** | Airship Guild Coordinator | Half-elf entrepreneur, 120 years in the trade. Believes aerial perspective reveals patterns no ground-level observer can see. Thinks in decades. |
| **Jin "Three-Rivers" Nakamura** | River Trading Consortium | Human diplomat, 45 years. Sees trade as the architecture of peace. More afraid of economic collapse than of any military threat. |
| **Marcus "Iron-Wheels" Thorne** | Overland Caravan Guild | Dwarf logistics master, 180 years. Conservative strategist who values proven routes over innovation. Has outlasted four Emperors. |
| **Kaia Saltborn** | Floating Market Coordinator | Triton cultural bridge, 85 years. Adapts Market operations to changing conditions. Integrates refugees into Market infrastructure as a matter of policy. |

The four operational paths — **Skyship merchants** working remote territories the ground routes can't reach, **River traders** threading the inland waterways, **Road caravans** serving the Empire's road network, **Tinker trains** providing specialized craft services across all territories — maintain their own routes and their own relationships. What connects them is the Compact.

## The Continuity Compact

The Markets' official philosophy is neutrality. Their actual philosophy is more specific: *no single faction should achieve total dominance, and information should move freely enough that no faction can engineer a crisis in isolation without the Markets detecting it first.*

This is called the Continuity Compact, and it is not something the Markets advertise. Within the operational structure, there are four hidden functions that serve the Compact's actual mission:

**The Memory Keepers** travel with the caravans carrying historical knowledge that exists nowhere else — records predating the Binding, accounts the Empire has been trying to erase, the kind of documentation that only survives because it kept moving.

**The Bridge-Builders** specialize in preventing factional conflicts from reaching the point of no return. They have stopped three wars in the last century through careful management of information flows between parties who were each acting on incomplete intelligence.

**The Current-Readers** analyze economic and political data from across all trade networks and produce assessments of developing crises that the Markets share internally — and occasionally, selectively, with the party best positioned to address what's coming.

**The Safe-Holders** maintain secure transit for materials too dangerous for any single faction to control. What they currently carry is not discussed outside the Circle.

## The Vault of Necessities

Hidden somewhere in the Market network — the location rotates on a schedule known only to the Circle — is a facility the Compact has maintained since shortly after the Binding: a repository of what the Empire has been trying to ensure doesn't survive.

It contains pre-Compact historical records predating the Imperial revision, cosmic cycle documentation tracking previous iterations of the Iremael, communications from the Bonfire Keep's Anchors preserved across centuries, and emergency protocols for maintaining functional civilization through the kind of collapse that the Markets believe is now approaching.

It also contains — and this is the detail the Circle does not share with anyone, including their own operational people — the only complete copy of the original Founding Compact, including clauses about Serenitas's imprisonment that the current Emperor's court does not know exist.

The Markets have been waiting for someone to need that copy for fourteen hundred years. They have strong opinions about who should be the first to read it.

# Beyond the Three

The three powers and the Markets account for most of the organized political life of Hameria Ire, but they are not the whole of it. Several smaller forces operate below the level of the great powers, each relevant to the current crisis in ways that range from directly dangerous to unexpectedly useful.

## Clan Mors

*The Skaven Assassination Clan. The shadow still reaching for what it lost.*

Clan Mors is an Old World institution — a Skaven assassination clan that has operated in the underways beneath major cities for generations, building its reputation on the specific methodology of taking promising subjects and reforging them into weapons through processes their training manuals call *graduation ceremonies* and their graduates call something else entirely. They specialize in living plague weapons: operatives whose presence becomes the affliction.

Their interest in Hameria Ire is specific. One of their graduates escaped. Skreek Swicschnout carries a plague arm that was meant to be a leash, and his survival among the surface world's populations represents a failure the clan cannot afford to leave unaddressed.

**Warlord Snikch the Unseen** leads Clan Mors from a position no surviving target has ever seen from. **Hunter-Captain Snakt** runs the recovery operations in Hameria Ire. The clan has been establishing underground infrastructure in neutral territories — hidden compounds, corrupted merchant cover, local assets in positions to report.

They are not a cosmic force. They are a long arm reaching for something that got away. In a campaign about the end of the world, Clan Mors is the reminder that some threats are simply personal.

## The Archive of Withered Echoes

*The Shadowfell repository for histories too dangerous to leave in the material world.*

The Archive exists within an obsidian spire that phases between layers of the Shadowfell, maintained by Shadar-kai chroniclers who have spent centuries developing the emotional discipline necessary to study histories of catastrophic failure without being destroyed by them. It was founded after early scholars discovered that certain knowledge — records of failed civilizations, forbidden rituals, collapsed timelines — required containment as much as preservation.

**High Chronicler Vespara the Eternal** has overseen the Archive for more than two thousand years. Her Shadar-kai longevity is not the source of her title; the depth of her engagement with the Archive's collections is.

The Archive's cosmic cycle documentation — records of previous Grand Harvests, evidence of prior Pregenitor interventions, accounts of endings both successful and failed — makes it potentially the most valuable research resource in existence for the current crisis. Access is restricted. Authorization is required. The Archive will not simply provide what it holds because someone asks.

One of the party's connections to the Archive is direct: Oriyen Vale served as its youngest chronicler in centuries before his departure. His access has not been formally revoked. His relationship with the institution is *complicated.*

## The Plague-Touched Network

*The underground mutual aid organization that Skreek Swicschnout didn't intend to lead but ended up leading.*

The Plague-Touched Network emerged from the practical necessity of people carrying supernatural afflictions — curses, plagues, magical conditions — who found themselves expelled from mainstream society and needed somewhere to be. It began as informal mutual support among outcasts and evolved, gradually, into a sophisticated underground organization with medical research, intelligence capabilities, safe houses, and now — following Skreek's appointment as official *Network Coordinator and Community Liaison for the Harvest Festival* — a degree of legitimate standing that its members find simultaneously useful and surreal.

The Network's value beyond its membership is its attention. Outcasts notice things that mainstream society ignores. They occupy the margins where early warning signs appear before they become crises. Members include **Pip**, who escaped Clan Mors experiments and can distinguish artificial corruption from natural affliction; **Old Martha**, whose herbal knowledge addresses conditions that official medicine refuses to categorize; and **Twist**, who specializes in exactly the assessments that the current situation keeps requiring.

It is, in its way, the smallest and most functional of Hameria Ire's organized forces. It has no armies and no political standing and very little that the three great powers would recognize as power. It has people who have survived things the great powers would prefer not to acknowledge, and it has been paying careful attention to what's been happening for a long time.

## The Redclaw Clan

*The goblin clan that consumed Norm Alfella's grief and found it made a better weapon than a wound.*

The Redclaw Clan was, before the current situation, a model of goblin pragmatism: survivors on the margins of human territory, careful rather than aggressive, maintaining their communities through scavenging and tactical retreat rather than confrontation. Then Norm arrived — an exiled human seeking refuge, carrying a history of trauma that the clan gradually absorbed as their own — and then Norm left, recognizing what his presence was doing, and the clan experienced his departure as abandonment rather than wisdom.

**Shaman Skarfang the Bitter** seized the leadership in the aftermath and transformed the accumulated resentment into purpose: systematic revenge against human settlements, raids designed for maximum psychological impact rather than practical gain, a new identity built on hatred instead of survival.

The corruption has found them. Several clan members report hearing beautiful voices in the quiet hours — voices that promise rest through ending, that validate the hatred as sacred purpose. **Snik the Listener** has been hearing these voices for months. The raids have become theatrical and symbolic in ways that fear alone cannot explain. The entropy is using the clan's grief the way it uses everything else: as a door.

The Redclaw Clan is a microcosm of what the Grand Harvest's overdue pressure does at the community level — taking real pain and offering false resolution, making destruction feel like meaning. Norm's connection to the clan makes it more than background. The question of whether there is a path to the clan's redemption is, among other things, a question about his.
