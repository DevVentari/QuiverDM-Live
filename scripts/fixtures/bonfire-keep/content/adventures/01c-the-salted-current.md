# The Salted Current

*Characters of levels 6–7. Maritime mystery and temporal horror in the Tidal Covenant's waters. The second lesson in what the Empire's light actually costs.*

---

## Background

The Tidal Covenant has been watching the northern currents slow for three centuries. They charted it, catalogued it, argued about it in every floating city from the Great Confluence to the Abyssal Shelf. The Drifters said keep moving. The Depth-Singers said listen to what the dense water is trying to say. The Anchors chained their cities to the seabed, which the Depth-Singers have been explaining, with increasing urgency, is accelerating the problem rather than solving it.

What none of them have been able to explain, even to themselves, is why.

The temporal debt — the mechanism by which the Binding pulls momentum from the world's oceans to supplement Serenitas's siphoned output when it falls short — has been compounding for fourteen centuries. The northern currents of the Great Bay are not slowing because of weather or geology. They are slowing because the Empire is borrowing time from them and has not, in fourteen centuries, paid any of it back.

Ships entering the dense zones emerge a week late. Creatures caught in the thickest concentrations become Salt-Statues: aware, immobile, suspended at the exact moment of contact with the density, eyes still tracking, still waiting, still inhabiting a present that is moving too slowly for the body to function in but too quickly for the mind to forget.

Current-Caller Nerida believes the Abyssal Shelf — the deep canyon where the currents pile up — is the accelerant's source. She needs people who can navigate temporal hazards and do not require a long explanation of who is probably responsible before they will act. The party, having demonstrated at the Bonfire Keep that they can be trusted with difficult realities, qualifies.

---

## Adventure Hooks

**The Missing Chronicler:** A contact of Oriyen's from the Shadowed Archive was aboard a trade vessel carrying pre-Binding Covenant navigational charts — the kind that document what the bay's currents looked like before the temporal debt began distorting them. The ship entered a Stagnation zone two weeks ago. The ship's arrival was logged, one week late, with no one aboard able to account for the missing time. The contact's cabin showed signs of sudden crystallization. The charts were gone.

**The Story Paused:** Faeren arrives at the Keep wearing the face of a Covenant dockworker — salt-cracked hands, specific laugh — and sits with the party long enough to say one thing that is not quite a warning and not quite a request. *A story is being paused in the Great Bay.* Stories, Faeren explains to anyone unfamiliar with their particular frame of reference, are what they run on. A story that cannot continue toward its ending is a story in which everyone in it is suspended. They find this intolerable. They would like someone to go and make it resume.

**The Market Problem:** The Wandering Markets are losing serious money to the maritime delays, and Jin "Three-Rivers" Nakamura is not a person who allows serious money to be lost without investigation. His contact on the ground — **Mira Saltborn**, a Triton freight coordinator who has been losing cargo to the Stagnation zones for months — makes the practical case: the hire is real, the payment is real, and the trade routes need to be open before the disruption becomes an economic crisis the great factions notice.

## Key Features

### The Great Confluence

The floating city is not calm. It is never entirely calm — the Covenant's civilization is built on the principle that motion is life, and the Great Confluence in full trade operation is a specific kind of organized chaos that visitors either find exhilarating or overwhelming — but this is different. The Drifters' ships are crowded at the cleaner docks, reluctant to push into the northern zones. In the distance, the chained cities of **Commodore Vax's** Anchor faction sit motionless — iron cables descending into the bay floor, the platforms refusing to drift. The Depth-Singers have been explaining for months that the Stagnation concentrates fastest around things that have stopped. The Anchors have heard this. Their chains remain. Merchants who have been waiting for cargo that was supposed to arrive two weeks ago are performing the specific mathematics of loss while shouting at whoever is nearest. The Current-Singers standing at the bay's edge are chanting in the resonant harmonics that should move clean water, and the water is not moving, and they are adapting their harmonics, and the adapted harmonics are not moving it either.

The Depth-Singers who have spent years listening to the dense zones are quieter than the merchants. They have the expressions of people who have been saying something is wrong for a very long time and have now reached the point where they would prefer to be less right.

**Environmental Effects:**
- **The Dense Water:** In Stagnant zones, creatures have advantage on saves against drowning but their movement speed is halved. Falling into the water doesn't cause damage; you simply "bounce" and get stuck in the surface tension.
- **Temporal Echoes:** Occasional flashes of the past or future appear in the mist — ships that haven't arrived yet, or the ruins of the city as it might look in a thousand years.

---

### The Abyssal Shelf

The canyon descends further than the diving bells' chains were designed to reach, which is why it takes a combination of Covenant equipment and magical breathing to get to the depth where the currents have stopped moving entirely. The water here has a quality that is not quite pressure and not quite cold — a thickness, a resistance, as if reality itself has developed a higher viscosity in this location. The Depth-Singers call it the deep density. They have been very careful not to spend too long here because of what happens to perception in zones where time is moving at a different rate.

Three things are in the deep.

**The Calcified Leviathan** — a creature of the deep bay, enormous, half-turned to salt-crystal, its body arrested mid-motion in a Stagnation event that must have happened years ago. It is not dead. Its pain is active and large and causing localized pressure waves that the narrow canyon amplifies into something between a tidal bore and an earthquake. It cannot leave. It cannot die. It is suspended in the moment of its crystallization and generating distress in all the water around it.

**The Flow-Thieves** — a faction of rogue Covenant mages who discovered, some months ago, that Stagnant-Time water can be harvested, bottled, and sold as a preservative of extraordinary effectiveness. They have been intentionally damming a secondary current channel to maintain a concentrated harvest zone, which has been compounding the Stagnation's severity in the northern bay. They are not evil by design — they found a resource, they extracted it, they made commercial decisions. The fact that the resource is borrowed time that belongs to the world's oceans is a philosophical problem they have been successfully not thinking about.

**The Rift of Stillness** — at the deepest accessible point of the Shelf, where the water has stopped flowing down as well as laterally, there is an opening. Not a cave. Not a geological feature. A *pause* in the ocean floor, a micro-tear in the barrier between temporal flow and temporal arrest, where the Binding's debt-collection has carved a wound in the world's architecture. This is not the Flow-Thieves' doing. This predates their operation. This is fourteen centuries of compound interest on a loan the ocean never agreed to take out, finding the thinnest point in the world's fabric and beginning to show through it.

The Rift is a precursor — a small version of what the Binding's eventual failure will look like at scale, in a contained location where it can still be addressed. Contained, for now.

---

## The Revelation

The Stagnation is not weather. It is not the ocean's natural behavior. It is not even the Flow-Thieves, though they have made it worse.

It is the Binding. The mechanism that imprisons Serenitas does not run on her alone — it draws on secondary sources when her siphoned output falls short, and the oceans of Hameria Ire are the largest available secondary source. Fourteen centuries of temporal momentum, pulled from the currents by an apparatus that has never had to ask permission, has accumulated as debt in the northern bay. The dense water is the debt made physical. The Salt-Statues are what happens to things that cannot pay a debt they didn't know they owed.

The Empire is not stealing life from just the Verdant Clans' forests. They are stealing *time* from the ocean, and the ocean does not have infinite time to give, and the Binding is still running, and the debt is still compounding.

Chronosian Elara has a number for this — a running total she has been filing with the Emperor for years, recommendations unanswered. The party does not know this yet. But the Rift of Stillness at the bottom of the Abyssal Shelf is visible evidence that her calculations are correct, and it can be documented, and documentation of this specific thing is among the most dangerous things in Hameria Ire's current political situation.

---

## Consequences

**Success:** The Flow-Thieves are disrupted. The secondary blockage clears. The Rift of Stillness can be sealed — temporarily, incompletely, buying time rather than solving the underlying cause. The northern currents resume at something closer to their pre-crisis rate. Nerida receives the party's report on the Rift and goes quiet in the way of someone who had suspected something and would have preferred to be wrong. The party now possesses evidence — physical, witnessed evidence — that the Empire's Binding is causing the Stagnation. Combined with what they found beneath Vaelora, the picture of what the Empire is actually doing is becoming clear.

**Failure:** The Stagnation zone expands to encompass the Great Confluence. The city's Drifter population evacuates into the cleaner water, but the platforms, the stationary infrastructure, the Anchor-faction cities that refused to move — these become nucleation points for the density. The Covenant loses its primary trade hub to a temporal pocket that will take years, if it ever resolves, to clear. The second great faction has been effectively neutralized before the real crisis begins.

**What the Party Now Knows:** They have now seen the same crime twice, from two different angles. In the Reach, the cost was biological — the Silver Rot, the crystallized groves, the silver veins in the druids' arms. In the bay, the cost is temporal — the dense water, the Salt-Statues, the Rift. Both are caused by the same mechanism. Both are accelerating. The Binding is not stable. The Empire says it is stable. The Empire has been saying it is stable for fourteen centuries.
