# Mid-Journey Encounters

*Levels 5–6. The descent from the Thornspines into the Withered world. The first sustained exposure to what the Binding costs.*

---

The world below the Thornspines does not look like the world ended. It looks like the world is holding very still, waiting to find out if it has to.

The Silver Rot does not announce itself with violence. It announces itself with the specific quality of wrongness that arrives when something alive stops moving and the air above it does not smell right. Trees that should be dropping leaves in autumn have leaves of metallic silver that do not fall — they simply stop where they are, each one catching the light like a coin. The grass in the lower passes crunches underfoot in a way that grass should not crunch. The birdsong has changed frequency, or gone entirely, or occasionally arrives from one direction and then immediately from another, slightly wrong in both cases.

The Verdant Clans have a word for this. It translates, approximately, as *the sound of the world forgetting how to continue.* They use it the way the Empire uses words for administrative failure — with precision, because precision is what you need when something has been going wrong for a long time and you are trying to communicate the specific degree.

The encounters on the road to the Reach do not all require fighting. Some of them require deciding.

## The Silver Drift

*Two hours below the mountain pass*

The snow has turned. Not all of it — only in a quarter-mile corridor where the Stillness has concentrated enough to convert precipitation — but the snow within the drift is the color of ground glass, and it settles differently, and the temperature inside it is the temperature of something that has been asked to be cold and has complied with a literalness that cold does not normally produce.

At the center of the drift: a family of mountain goats frozen mid-leap. Not dead — or not in any way that shows injury or decay. Simply arrested. Their eyes still track. When approached, the goats closest to the party turn their heads very slowly, following movement, their breath still producing faint vapor that drifts upward and then stops. They are suspended in a moment that is continuing at a rate too slow for their biology to function in, and they cannot get out of it, and they have been here long enough that the grass below their hooves has crystallized around them.

Touching the nearest goat produces a sound: a low, sustained harmonic from the glass beneath its frozen hooves, as if the Stillness is tuned to a specific frequency and physical contact completes a circuit.

Characters passing through the drift must succeed on a **DC 14 Constitution save** each hour or gain a level of Exhaustion as the Stillness begins to pattern-match their body's movement to its own rhythm. Verdant tracking expertise or the Keystone of the Keep reduces this DC to 10.

## The Imperial Patrol

*The lower road junction, midday*

The patrol found the refugees about two hours ago and has not decided what to do with them yet. This is visible in the arrangement: Imperial soldiers in formation, a **Golden Siphon-Warden** with his calibration device activated, and three Verdant families sitting on their packs at the road's edge with the specific stillness of people who have been told to wait and have been waiting long enough to stop expecting the wait to end pleasantly.

The Warden is scanning the refugees. The calibration beam — golden, precise — is not hurting them visibly, but the way the youngest child is holding her arm after the scan has passed suggests that *visibly* is doing significant work in that sentence.

The Warden's stated purpose is identifying "unregistered essence signatures" — a protocol that has been running for approximately three weeks, since someone in the Imperial administrative hierarchy decided that the Pregenitor artifacts were a threat to Binding stability and issued orders to locate and catalog them. He has not, he will say if asked, been given guidance on what to do after he identifies them. This is clearly causing him professional discomfort that he is not sure how to express to people with swords.

If a party member uses an **Awakened Pregenitor Artifact** within range of the calibration device, the Warden's equipment activates a secondary alert mode with the involuntary clarity of a device doing its job without asking anyone's permission. "**Unregistered essence detected,**" it announces, in the flat carry of Imperial voice-casting, audible to everyone in the immediate area. "*Secure the catalyst.*" The Warden looks like he did not want that to happen. The soldiers look at the Warden. The refugees look at the party.

*Enemies:* 1 **Golden Siphon-Warden** (CR 6, 80 HP, Multiattack 1, deals +1d6 Radiant damage) and 3 **Imperial Solar-Guards** (Veteran stats, +1d6 Radiant damage).

*Alternatively:* The patrol can be talked past, redirected, or made to consider the refugees' situation as the more immediate problem. The Warden is operating on orders he did not write and is not certain he agrees with. He has a child's age in mind — his own, or someone else's, something that makes the youngest refugee's expression difficult to hold without it costing something. This is not weakness. It is the specific access point available in someone doing something they know is wrong because they have not yet found the path to doing otherwise.

## The Mirror of the Past

*The forest's edge, a frozen pond*

The pond froze in a manner that ponds do not freeze. The ice is horizontal but not cloudy — it is the specific transparency of **Salt-Glass**, which means it does not reflect the sky above it but instead reveals what sits below it, and what sits below it is not the pond's floor.

The images in the glass are of this landscape fourteen hundred years ago. The same hills, the same bend in the road — but inhabited by a world that does not know what is coming, in which the trees are forty feet taller and growing, in which something vast and warm moves at the treeline that has not moved at any treeline in recent memory. Looking at the image produces the sensation common to all glass-based reflection phenomena of this type: not nostalgia, which is warm, but the cooler thing — the recognition of what was lost at sufficient remove that the recognition is almost abstract.

The **Salt-Statue Echo** rises from the pond's edge as the party approaches. It is the preserved impression of someone who was caught in a Stagnation event and has been here since — not a ghost, not a revenant, but a pattern sustained by the same temporal density that turned the pond's water into glass. It does not threaten. It orients toward whoever carries a mirror-resonant artifact and says, in a voice that carries the flattened quality of someone speaking from inside a stopped moment: "You are recording the wrong ending. The song should have finished centuries ago." If the party engages it — speaks to it, asks it questions, simply remains present without attacking — it will provide one piece of intelligence about the **Imperial Twelve** before the temporal density that sustains it disperses into the afternoon air.

*Encounter:* 1 **Salt-Statue Echo** (CR 5). It does not initiate violence. If attacked, it defends briefly and then disperses without completing the dissolution — it cannot hold form against sustained aggression. The intelligence it holds is lost with it.

## The Thorn Ambush

*A narrow canyon, late afternoon*

The canyon is where the Thorns prefer to work. The root-walls make restraint easy — a DC 15 Dexterity save when the first motion triggers the activation, or be held by something that has been growing into the stone here for three centuries and knows exactly how deep the grip needs to be.

The **Thorns** are the Verdant faction that the Clans do not officially acknowledge and Theron has not been asked directly about. They believe the grove-network's current degradation is a direct result of Verdant cooperation with external factions, and they are not entirely wrong. They have been watching the road to the Reach for two weeks and have reached the conclusion that anyone approaching with Imperial-style equipment is either a threat or an opportunity depending on what they say in the first thirty seconds.

The leader is a masked elf with silver veins to her collarbone, which she displays without apparent self-consciousness — the silver, in Thorn symbology, is proof of investment. She wants to know who the party works for. She expects a lie. She has a specific look for people who are not lying that she has developed over several years of ambushing people on this road, and it is difficult to perform convincingly.

**DC 16 Persuasion** to convince her that the party was drawn by the Heartflame rather than commissioned by the **Twelve Witnesses** or any Imperial proxy. If this succeeds, she does something that the Thorn protocols do not technically authorize: she hands over a map with a bypass route marked that avoids an Imperial blockade three miles ahead. The blockade has been there for ten days. The Thorns have been routing refugees around it with this map. She has not decided yet whether giving it to the party is the same category of action. She gives it anyway.

*Enemies:* 4 **Root-Bound Deserters** (CR 2) and 1 **Thorn Stalker** (Assassin stats, poison damage replaced with Necrotic). The restraining roots release on a successful DC 15 Strength check or 15 slashing damage to the root at the character's location.

## The Glass-Blighted Village

*A small Verdant settlement, an hour from the Reach*

Ninety percent of the village is glass. The transformation is precise in a way that decay is not — each crystallized structure perfectly preserves the moment it arrested, mid-meal on one table, mid-repair on a roof, mid-song with a carved instrument held in hands that can no longer move. The remaining ten percent, one intact house at the village's center, is being held by a single druid who has been awake for four days.

She is turning to glass from the feet up. It is past her ankles. She is kneeling in the house's center with both hands pressed to the floor, and through the preserved window the party can see what the floor is connected to: a network of root-filaments extending throughout the village, and at the far ends of the filaments, five children who are not glassed yet because she is using herself as a buffer between the Stillness and them.

She knows what she is doing. She made the calculation clearly and some time ago. What she needs is not rescue but supplementation — something to add to the equation that changes the arithmetic of how long she has.

Characters can choose to charge the druid's working with their **Pregenitor Artifacts**, feeding her the energy she needs to hold the buffer until someone can evacuate the children properly. This saves the village's survivors. The cost: the artifacts carry a **−1 penalty to attack rolls and spell save DCs** until the party reaches the Heart of Vaelora, where the Heartflame's ember can be used to restore them. It is not a small cost in what is coming. It is also the thing in front of them right now.

She does not ask. She is past asking. She would not ask anyone to pay this price, which is why she has been paying it herself. The party can observe the situation, understand the mathematics, and decide.

---

*By the time the Emerald Reach's canopy closes overhead and the light goes green and the static begins — the first brush of the Silver frequency against the edges of spellcasting, the particular silence where birdsong should be — the party has seen enough to know that this is not a local problem. The road showed them. Every mile of it showed them. The Reach is where the source is. But the source is not the Reach.*

*It never was.*
