# Whispered Names

*Characters of levels 3–4. The trial of the Thornspine Mountains. The first test.*

---

## Background

The Heartflame does not send invitations. It sends weather.

The storm that rises in the Thornspine Mountains ahead of certain travelers is not a natural phenomenon and does not behave like one — snow falling sideways against the wind's direction, drifts forming in geometric patterns no cold front produces, a cold that seems less interested in killing than in *stopping*. Those who have felt it describe, afterward, a sense of being assessed. Of something vast and patient measuring the space between who they are and who they have been afraid to become.

The Bonfire Keep sits at the end of this storm. Not past it — through it. The trial is the door.

It has three stages. First: the cairns, where what was buried surfaces. Second: the stones, where what was wanted meets what was chosen. Third: the guardian at the threshold, which is the storm's last argument against the idea that these particular people are worth the fire's investment.

The Anchors do not administer the trial. The Heartflame administers the trial. The Anchors have simply been on the other side of the door for a very long time, waiting to see who makes it through.

---

## Adventure Hooks

These travelers do not need to know each other. They do not need to have set out together. The storm finds people in different places and herds them toward the same pass with the patient thoroughness of something that has done this before and is not in a hurry.

- The party is crossing the Thornspine range — through commerce, through pursuit, through necessity, through the specific kind of desperation that makes a high mountain pass in winter seem like a reasonable route. The storm rises without warning on a clear morning.
- A dying traveler, found in a roadside shelter days before, pressed a folded map into someone's hands and managed three words before losing consciousness: *don't stop walking.* The map leads into the Thornspine range. The marked path seems to lead nowhere.
- Someone in the party has been having the same dream for weeks — a fire in a hearth, amber and occasionally deep blue, smelling like something specific they cannot name. The dream is not frightening. It is *insistent.*
- A merchant who survived the pass three months ago describes the storm in terms that don't quite add up. Didn't last long enough. Wasn't cold enough to kill. Felt, she says, like it was *listening.*

---

## Arrival

The storm manifests without warning on what begins as a clear mountain day. Characters traveling the high passes of the Thornspine Mountains find themselves engulfed in a blizzard that rises from the valleys below — not sweeping in from the horizon but *rising*, as if the mountain itself exhaled it. The temperature drops in seconds. Snow moves sideways, then upward, then in spiraling currents that don't correspond to any wind. The light flattens to a gray-white directionlessness that makes it genuinely difficult to determine which way is down.

And then the whispers start.

Not a single voice. A specific one — for each traveler individually — speaking in a language they somehow understand despite the dialect being one they have never heard before. It knows their names. Not the names they use. The ones they gave themselves in the dark, the ones they carry in the parts of themselves they don't introduce to people. It is not threatening. It is simply *accurate*, and accuracy in this context is its own category of frightening.

**Environmental Effects:**
- Visibility reduced to 15 feet
- Difficult terrain due to shifting, unnatural snow
- DC 13 Survival checks every 10 minutes to maintain bearings
- Personal whispers targeting each character's greatest regrets
- Supernatural cold that threatens exhaustion

The warmth, when it comes, arrives before any visible source explains it — a sensation of amber-colored heat against the left side of the face, like a hearth through a wall. *That way.* Not a voice. A direction.

---

## Key Features

### The Three Cairns of Regret

**Location:** Deep within the storm, each marked by a sphere of warm golden light that the blizzard curves around without touching

The cairns are old — older than the pass, older than the roads, built in architectural styles that don't correspond to any living culture's records. They are not burial markers, though they look like them. They are the places where the storm's attention sharpens into specificity. Where the whispers stop being general and become *personal*.

Each cairn has been waiting for a specific person. It does not advertise this. It simply contains, resting in a hollow at its base, an object that belongs to someone in the party — not an object they have seen before, but one they *recognize*.

**The Scholar's Cairn** — Precise geometric stone, the angles mathematical in a way that feels archival, like something that was designed to hold information. Resting at its base: a shard of obsidian mirror, dark as held breath. When held, it shows not the holder's reflection but *moments* — specific choices where observation was chosen over action, where presence would have changed something, rendered with the specific clarity of memory that has never been processed into distance. It asks nothing. It simply shows.

**The Healer's Cairn** — Stacked in formations that suggest something between architecture and warning, marked at each joint with dark runes in no legible script. Resting at its base: a shard of warp-stone, green-black, pulsing with a rhythm that is not quite a heartbeat. For someone carrying an affliction they were told was a weapon — something given to them rather than chosen — it reads differently than it would for anyone else. It does not pulse with threat. It pulses with *question*.

**The Survivor's Cairn** — Forms a rough circle, one stone placed deliberately outside the perimeter in exclusion. Resting within the circle's center: a vest, its fabric wrong in a way that takes a moment to identify — woven from something that is not quite thread, fibres of unmade possibility, the material remnant of choices that were never taken. It should look like nothing. It looks, to the right person, like everything they have ever found and kept when someone else would have left it behind. For someone who has been outside a circle — who knows the specific sensation of the stone placed separate — it fits in the way that things fit people who were not supposed to have them. Precisely. Without trying.

**Encounter:** When a character touches their corresponding relic, they face their **Spectral Regret** — the moment they most wish had gone differently, given weight and presence and the ability to swing back. Use Spectre statistics, modified so Life Drain cannot reduce characters below 1 HP. Only the character who triggered it can see their Spectre; allies can provide Help actions, fighting blind against something they cannot see but can hear their companion struggling with.

The Spectre is not trying to kill them. It is trying to be *acknowledged*.

---

### The Three Stones of Choice

**Location:** Deeper in the storm, arranged in a perfect equilateral triangle, each stone seven feet of dark obsidian

After the cairns, the storm pulls back slightly — not ending, but creating a negative space around the standing stones that allows visibility to extend perhaps thirty feet. The stones are dark, apparently inert. Then the relics in the party's hands begin to pulse with the same rhythm as the stones, and the obsidian surfaces light from within: one red as coals, one blue as the deep ocean, one the warm gold of the Heartflame's specific color.

Each stone offers something. The storm offers something else — voices from outside the circle, pulling toward urgency, toward need, toward *there, someone needs you, come away from here*. The voices may or may not be telling the truth. The storm is not above lying. It is a test, after all.

**The Power Stone (Crimson)** — Grants +2 damage to all attacks and advantage on Strength checks

**The Wisdom Stone (Azure)** — Grants +2 to attack rolls with finesse/monk weapons and advantage on Investigation/Insight checks

**The Unity Stone (Golden)** — Allows Help action as a reaction within 30 feet and advantage on Charisma checks

**Encounter:** Characters lured away from the stone circle by storm whispers face **3 Dire Wolves** emerging from the blizzard, hunting by separation — they do not rush headlong but attempt to split the group, using knockdown rather than kill tactics, as if they are trying to demonstrate what happens to things that don't hold together.

**Permanent Benefits:** Characters who carve their names into a chosen stone gain lasting enhancements to their relics and special abilities usable at the Bonfire Keep.

---

### The Final Trial: Ice Elemental Guardian

**Location:** The last hundred yards of approach to the Bonfire Keep, where the storm makes its final argument

The Bonfire Keep is visible from here — or at least the light of it is visible, amber bleeding through the white, a warmth that the cold cannot fully explain away. Between the party and that warmth: a form assembled from the storm itself, ice and snow and compressed air given enough coherence to stand and swing and speak.

It does not fight viciously. It fights *specifically* — speaking directly to each character as it engages them, naming things the whispers identified at the cairns, asking whether the proof of the cairn was genuine or performance. Whether growth chosen under pressure is actually growth or just pressure taking a different shape. The questions are not comfortable. Neither is the cold.

But the storm is weakening as the Keep draws them closer. The guardian is fighting the Heartflame's pull as much as it is fighting the party, and it is losing ground even before the first blow lands.

**Encounter:** Modified **Large Elemental** (90 HP) with special mechanics:

- **Storm's End:** Loses 5 HP each round as the storm weakens
- **Unity Strike:** Coordinated attacks by 2+ characters gain +2 damage
- **Character Victory Conditions:** Individual achievements (helping allies, making contact for healing, standing exposed to attacks) grant special benefits and demonstrate personal growth

## Consequences

**Success:** The storm collapses. The Bonfire Keep's door opens. Temmel is there — he has been there, standing near the door, because he is always near the door, because he knows what someone who has run out of options looks like and he has been trying to be the person who meets them for fourteen centuries. The Three Anchors have been waiting. The fire has been burning. The party has just proven that the Calling was right about them. What comes next is the rest of it.

**Partial Success:** The party reaches the Keep, but the confrontations at the cairns were incomplete — truths acknowledged but not absorbed, wounds touched but not faced. The Anchors receive them with full hospitality. The deeper mysteries of the Keep, and the Anchors' full confidence, wait for the party to be ready to finish what the storm started.

**Failure:** The storm does not let go. Travelers who cannot move forward find themselves circling — the pass looking the same from every direction, the whispers growing less specific and more constant, the cold settling in not as threat but as *invitation*. The storm has all the time in the world. It has been here longer than the roads. It will be here after. It is not cruel. It is simply patient in the way that fire is patient, and in the Thornspine Mountains, the distinction between a test and a trap depends entirely on whether you're still walking.
