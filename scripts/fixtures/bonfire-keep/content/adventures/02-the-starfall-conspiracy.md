# The Starfall Conspiracy

*Characters of levels 8–9. Cosmic crisis and diplomatic horror in Concordia Stellaris. The third lesson in what the Empire's maintenance actually requires.*

---

## Background

The seven-year alignment of Thaelis and Morwyn is not simply a festival.

The Sunward Empire has understood this for fourteen hundred years, and that understanding has never been offered to the faction leaders who attend, the diplomats who negotiate, or the thousands who travel from across Hameria Ire to witness the twin moons achieve their closest approach. The Harvest Festival's public purpose — the pageantry, the peace renewal, the generations of cautious diplomacy between three great civilizations — is real. But beneath it runs a second ceremony, private, precise, and non-negotiable: a ritual that uses the alignment's amplified resonance to stabilize the Binding's secondary channels.

The Aetheric Well holds Serenitas. The Binding keeps the Well sealed. But the Binding, like any working mechanism running fourteen centuries past its intended lifespan, has developed stress fractures — places where the siphoning pressure has worn through the original architecture, places that require periodic reinforcement if the whole apparatus is to continue. The Sacred Amphitheater's star-metal bowl was not constructed for the festival. It was constructed for this ceremony. Every seven years, when the alignment brings the moons to their maximum resonance, the Empire uses that amplification to reinforce what is fraying.

Without the renewal, the secondary channels destabilize. The siphon runs unmodulated. And when power of that scale runs unmodulated through a system that was never designed to run at this draw for this long, the result is not liberation — it is rupture.

Captain Marcus Draven, the Emperor's most trusted Hand, has been hearing a voice for six months. It does not announce itself as malevolent. It presents itself as exhausted — as something that has been waiting, in the dark, for someone patient enough to listen. It has been feeding him certainty in careful doses: that the ceremony is wrong, that the Binding's real cost is being hidden from him, that the most merciful act a loyal servant could perform is to let the machine stop. He is not corrupted in the way of obvious things. He is corrupted in the way of someone who has been told a true thing in the service of an action that the truth does not actually justify.

During the ceremony, at the moment of maximum alignment, he will strike.

## Adventure Hooks

**The Emperor's Invitation:** The Emperor has sent personal invitations to the party — not through intermediaries, not through the standard festival channels, but through Temmel, who delivered each one by hand and said only, with his particular economy of words, that the festival this year has qualities the Emperor would find it useful to have witnessed by people capable of distinguishing what is actually happening from what is being performed.

**Faeren's Story-Problem:** Faeren arrives at the Keep three weeks before the festival wearing the face of someone's grandmother and says, in that grandmother's voice, that a story is approaching its ending before it has finished. A particular story about a soldier who was told something true by the wrong voice and has made a terrible decision on the basis of it. Stories that end before they finish leave scars in the world, and Faeren has watched this world accumulate scars for fourteen centuries and finds each new one heavier than the last. They suggest — they always suggest, never command — that someone be in Concordia Stellaris when the moons align.

**The Mirror's Cold:** For weeks before the festival, the Fragment of the Obsidian Mirror has been cold. Not the cold of disuse — the cold of presence, the specific temperature of something watching from the other side. Brief flashes in the reflective surface: a tower recognizable to anyone with knowledge of the Shadowed Archive, columns of text in a hand that wasn't the one doing the looking, and once — just once, for only a moment — something vast and serpentine disappearing behind the edge of the frame. The Shadowed Archive keeps its files. Whatever has been watching has been studying them.

## Key Features

### Concordia Stellaris

The neutral city meets the party at its gates with noise and color and the specific organized chaos of a celebration that three faction governments have spent months ensuring will work flawlessly. Which is why the small wrongnesses are visible to anyone paying attention.

The Sacred Flame — the eternal torch at the city's center, burning since the first Harvest Festival, never extinguished — is flickering. Not dramatically. Just more than it should, in a space with no wind. The flowers in Thymal's Garden are in their brightest possible bloom, with the particular desperation of things blooming hard because something in them knows it won't last. The star-metal of the Sacred Amphitheater, which should hold the cold light of the moons like a mirror, carries a faint tarnish no one can explain.

Faction leaders are present. Most are performing contentment. The ones who are not performing — Current-Caller Nerida, who has not stopped watching the sky; Grove-Speaker Theron, whose hands have not left the ground since he arrived — have something else in their faces. The expression of people who have been saying something is wrong for a long time and have arrived at the festival because running out of ways to say it is not the same as being wrong.

Ambric is here. Faeren is here, in several faces simultaneously. Neither has attended the festival in living memory. The diplomatic ripples this causes are considerable. The reason for their attendance has not been asked directly by anyone with the standing to ask it.

**Investigation Opportunities:**
- **DC 15 Investigation:** Members of the Plague-Touched Network are positioned throughout the crowd, their signals tracking something systematically toward the Imperial pavilions.
- **DC 18 Medicine:** Several festival workers show early entropy corruption signs — the particular stillness, the faint tarnish to the skin, the slight delay between thought and action.
- **DC 16 Insight:** The pattern of affected individuals is not random. Someone has been choosing targets by proximity to the ceremony's anchor points.
- **DC 20 Perception:** Brief glimpses of Withering Remnants near the Imperial pavilions — not yet attacking, circling the edges of perception as if waiting for a signal.

### The Garden Dinner

The formal diplomatic dinner in Thymal's Garden is where personal stakes crystallize beneath the social performance. The food is impeccable. The flowers are too bright. The conversation is careful in the way of people who are all carrying something they have decided not to name at dinner.

**Emperor Aurelios** arrives late and goes directly to whoever is examining the mirror: "Your reputation precedes you. I wonder — have you ever documented something you later wished you hadn't? The weight of knowing things isn't always distributed fairly." He knows about the Archive. He has read the chronicles. He has not yet decided whether their presence here is a resource or a liability.

**Captain Marcus Draven** sits two seats away and excuses himself when his hand begins to tremble reaching for wine. The specific look — haunted, resolved, grief that has finished its argument with itself — is recognizable to anyone who has seen it before.

**High Sage Lyria** almost confides in someone about "containment protocols" before catching herself. She has been trying to give this particular confession for two days. Something keeps stopping her — not fear exactly, but the specific paralysis of someone who understands that once she says it, the world will be different afterward, and she is not entirely sure the world is ready to be different.

**Current-Caller Nerida** pulls someone aside to ask about the Imperial guard's behavior. She noticed it two days ago. She has been watching it ever since. She is hoping to be told she's imagining it.

That night, in the mirror: Marcus striking the Emperor. And behind Marcus's shoulder, barely visible, something vast and serpentine whispering into his ear. The thing is using words drawn from a chronicle that was written years ago. It has been reading the documentation. It knows exactly where the bloodline is vulnerable, because the bloodline's vulnerabilities were thoroughly recorded.

### The Sacred Amphitheater

The star-metal bowl was built for one purpose disguised as another, and the architecture knows it. Every angle calculated to channel the moons' light toward the central dais, every acoustic curve designed to carry the ritual harmonics outward through the valley. At peak alignment, it does not merely amplify. It *transforms*: magic cast inside the bowl during the ceremony is not the same magic that would exist elsewhere. The boundaries between moments thin. What has happened and what will happen and what is presently happening press against each other in the same space.

When the ceremony goes right, it is extraordinary.

**Cosmic Amplification Effects (Seven-Year Peak):**
- All spell damage doubled during perfect alignment
- Spell save DCs increased by +3
- Critical hits occur on 19-20 for all combatants
- Wild Magic surges on spells 3rd level or higher
- Temporal echoes: past and future events briefly overlay the present
- Planar barriers thin to gossamer — Withering Remnants and other planar creatures can manifest without summoning

**Key NPCs at the Ceremony:**
- **Emperor Aurelios:** Visibly strained, beginning the binding renewal with the precision of a man performing a critical procedure he has performed seven times before and is not certain he will complete
- **Captain Marcus Draven:** Fighting it to the last moment — hand trembling, jaw set, standing at his post because he has not yet accepted what his body already knows he has decided
- **High Sage Lyria:** Positioned at the ritual's outer anchor point, watching the channels with the expression of someone praying her calculations are wrong
- **Grove-Speaker Theron:** Sensing the death-adjacent wrongness from twenty feet away; has been sensing it since the flowers started blooming too hard
- **Current-Caller Nerida:** Positioned near the exits, not because she was told to, because she looked at the amphitheater and made a decision about where she would rather be when something happens

### The Assassination Climax

The twin moons achieve perfect alignment. The star-metal sings. The Emperor begins the renewal.

Draven moves.

*Modified Draven — Enhanced by Cosmic Alignment:*
- +3 attack bonus; doubled damage during alignment
- Void-Touched Blade: 2d8+8 slashing + 4d6 necrotic, ages target 2d4 years
- DC 19 Constitution save or drop to 0 HP on hit
- **Redemption path:** The corruption told him true things to justify a false conclusion. The right words — ones that acknowledge what the voice showed him rather than dismissing it — can reach him. He is not a villain. He is someone who was handed a real grievance and pointed at the wrong response.

The interrupted ceremony does not simply fail. The channels it was reinforcing experience a sudden, uncontrolled feedback pulse. The discharge tears a wound fifty feet above the dais — a hole the size of a wagon wheel, ragged at the edges, through which nothing coherent is visible, only pressure and hunger.

It expands.

**Reality Tear Mechanics:**
- Round 1: 10-foot diameter — Strength save DC 15 or pulled 5 feet toward it
- Round 2: 20-foot diameter — Strength save DC 17 or pulled 10 feet; the Knowledge Serpent emerges
- Round 3: 30-foot diameter — Strength save DC 19 or pulled 10 feet
- Round 4+: 40-foot diameter — no save, creatures in range automatically lifted
- Closing: requires 3 successful DC 18 Arcana checks plus spell slot expenditure; each failure risks permanent planar instability

**The Knowledge Serpent** (CR 12 — targets the mirror's bearer first)

It comes through the Tear like something that has been waiting very long for a door to open.

Serpentine, vast in a way that does not obey the geometry of the space it occupies, made of something between shadow and stolen text — columns of script running through its scales in a hand that will be recognized by anyone who spent time in the Shadowed Archive. It speaks in the voices of archivists who died there. It has been watching from the other side of every mirror touched by the Chronicle's Fragment since the Archive, and it is not patient anymore.

*"Thank you, little scribe,"* it says, in the voice of someone's first archivist mentor. *"You documented everything so thoroughly. The Bloodline Chronicles of House Aurelius — you made it so easy to find where the line was weak."*

It did not corrupt Draven with malice. It corrupted him with accuracy. It used the chronicles to identify every true grievance the man had, and arranged those grievances into a shape pointing at a door that should not be opened. The documentation was precise. The conclusion was not.

Its true target is the Emperor — to consume the imperial bloodline's specific magical resonance, which would give it direct access to the Binding's architecture.

## The Revelation

High Sage Lyria has been trying to confess for two days.

She knows what the ceremony actually does — not the public version, but the working mechanism beneath it. She knows the Binding's secondary channels run through this amphitheater, that every seven-year festival has carried this second ceremony beneath its public face without the faction leaders being told, and that the calculation Chronosian Elara has been filing with the Emperor for years — about what happens when a system at this extraction level approaches its maintenance threshold — has been received and acknowledged and set aside.

The Binding is not stable. The Emperor knows. The Binding's maintenance has become the first priority around which every other consideration is arranged, and the festival is one of the mechanisms by which that arrangement stays invisible.

What the party now holds, between the Tear and Draven and the Serpent and Lyria's confession, is a complete picture. Not just that the Empire is extracting from the world's foundations, but that they know, and have known, and have decided that continuing is preferable to what stopping would look like.

As the crisis peaks — through the damaged channels, through the thinned barriers — something vast and compressed and very far away makes a sound that is not quite a voice. It is not free. It is not speaking. It is simply, for just a moment, aware that the walls are thinner than usual, that somewhere above the Well something has gone briefly, terribly wrong, and that the people who built the cage are scrambling to repair it.

The moons continue their alignment. The moment passes. The world holds its next breath.

## Consequences

**Success:** The Tear is closed — barely, with damage that will require monitoring. The Serpent retreated through it carrying a clearer map of what it was trying to reach. Draven can be subdued rather than killed; what was done to him is documented, and documentation of it matters. The ceremony's reinforcement is incomplete. The Binding's secondary channels are damaged. Lyria's confession cannot be put back.

**Failure:** The Tear becomes permanent. The Serpent reaches the Emperor. The Binding's secondary channels, without their reinforcement, begin degrading with the particular inevitability of a load-bearing structure weakened and left under load. Concordia Stellaris becomes the site of the festival where the world stopped pretending everything was fine.

**What the Party Now Knows:** The evidence from Vaelora and the Great Bay was material and witnessed. Lyria's confession is institutional and documented. The picture is large enough now that it cannot be called a local problem. This is not a kingdom's dysfunction. It is fourteen centuries of a structure running past its design capacity, and the stress fractures are becoming visible in ways that even the Empire cannot hide. The Pregenitor Fragments exist. The First Covenant was made. The question the party will now carry is not whether the Binding is failing — it is whether, when it does, there is anything left that can answer the door.
