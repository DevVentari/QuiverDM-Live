# Desperate Alliance

*Characters of level 10. Three factions who have spent centuries perfecting the art of not trusting each other must learn to share a table in the shadow of the end of the world.*

---

## Background

The festival grounds of Concordia Stellaris were not designed to hold grief at this scale. They were designed for ceremony — for the precise and beautiful work of nations performing harmony at each other across a cleared field. What stands on those grounds now is something different. The canvas of the war camp tents does not quite sit right over the altar stones. The supply lines cut through where the processional path used to be. The corruption on the horizon makes the light wrong in a way that everyone notices and nobody says aloud.

Three weeks have passed since the Heartflame ceremony fractured the sky. The corruption now covers half of Hameria Ire, and the rate of spread is no longer the slow creep that allowed for measured responses. It has become something that requires the word *accelerating* and then requires sitting with what that word means. The three great factions — the **Verdant Circle**, the **Imperial Covenant**, and the **Ocean Covenant** — have never in recorded history shared a command structure. They are sharing one now because the alternative is watching the world die in their separate jurisdictions.

The discovery of the **Pregenitor Orb** fragments represents the only available axis of hope. The fragments are scattered — concealed in realms beyond ordinary reach, guarded by remnants of the previous cosmic cycle — and they must be claimed before the corruption reaches what the monitoring stations are calling critical mass. The first fragment is attainable. The expedition to claim it requires coordination that the factions are not, structurally or temperamentally, built to provide. This is the problem the party inherits when they arrive at the war camp.

What the party does not yet fully understand — though they may sense it in the way the faction leaders watch them — is that their cosmic significance has preceded them. The Pregenitors of the previous cycle are the reason these fragments exist as they do, guarded as they are, claimable only by those who carry the right resonance. The faction leaders are not treating the party as assets. They are treating them as the thing the assets are being organized around. The distinction matters. It means that what happens in the war council is not merely political theater — it is the party's first real test of whether they can hold the weight of that designation.

The assault will come before the expedition departs. The **Entropy Aspect** has been watching the alliance form, and a coordinated alliance is the one development it cannot afford. What happens during the assault will do more to determine the faction relationships going forward than any council decision. Suffering together, defending the same civilians, pulling each other out of void-touched visions — this is the language that centuries of political mistrust does not have a counter to.

## Adventure Hooks

**Called to the Table:** A courier bearing all three faction seals arrives at wherever the party is resting with a message that is three sentences long and carries the specific gravity of a document that was not easy to agree on. The message requests their presence at the Alliance War Council at first light. The courier does not know what the council will ask, but she knows they have been arguing for two days and stopped when someone suggested sending for the party.

**The Corruption Report:** A junior Imperial cartographer finds the party and shows them his latest mapping work without being asked — he has been showing it to everyone, compulsively, because he cannot stop looking at it and cannot stop needing other people to understand what the numbers mean. The line marking the corruption's edge is three days closer than last week's line. The line from the week before is three days closer than the line before that. He wants someone to tell him the pattern does not mean what it looks like it means.

**Theron's Summons:** **Grove-Speaker Theron** sends a single line through a medium that suggests significant deliberate effort to use: *The party should be present when we decide. They will carry what we decide toward. That is not a courtesy — it is a structural requirement.* No greeting. No signature. The botanical seal is pressed so hard into the wax that the impression has cracked.

## Key Features

### The Alliance War Council

*Central tent complex, festival grounds*

The main coordination tent is the size of a manor hall and feels smaller than it is because of what has been put inside it. Imperial efficiency has organized the logistics wall — supply counts, troop deployments, resource tallies in columns that are exact to the unit. The Verdant section has brought living matter into the space: root-anchored furniture, a canopy of preserved nightbloom that produces a faint cool light independent of the lanterns, the smell of deep forest that sits wrong against the canvas. The Covenant section is minimal in a way that reads less as restraint than as the careful preservation of space — water bowls at the cardinal points, the current maps rendered in salt on treated cloth, everything oriented toward movement.

Three tables have been pushed together into a triangle. The gap where they meet is noticeable. No one has mentioned it.

The council has been in session long enough that the tea has been replaced twice and the civility is beginning to show its seams. The core dispute is strategic rather than temperamental, which makes it worse — each faction's position is internally coherent, which means the argument cannot be resolved by finding the error. The **Empire** wants the **Sunward Wastes** — a large, equipped expedition with clear lines of command, leveraging military infrastructure. The **Verdant Circle** wants the **Dreaming Deep** — a smaller, adaptive group, leveraging long knowledge of places that do not behave the way places are supposed to. The **Ocean Covenant** wants the **Glass Sea** — multiple simultaneous expeditions, distributing risk, leveraging the Covenant's capacity for dispersed coordination. All three positions are defensible. All three positions, if adopted without the others, will cause friction that costs time the alliance does not have.

- **Unanimous Decision:** Maximum resource allocation — all three factions contribute specialized equipment, logistical support, and personnel. The expedition departs with everything it needs.
- **Majority Decision (2-1):** Strong resource allocation, but the dissenting faction holds something back — not as sabotage, but as a hedge against what they see as strategic error. The friction is survivable.
- **Split Decision:** The expedition departs with faction relationships damaged. Resources are adequate. The sense that this alliance might not hold becomes something everyone carries.

The party can mediate. **High Sage Lyria** responds to procedural clarity — she has been managing court disagreements for twenty-two years and there is a specific approach to her that works, which involves giving her a role in the resolution rather than a position in the argument. **Grove-Speaker Theron** will not be rushed and should not be argued with directly — he has four millennia of evidence for his perspective and knows it, but he will revise a conclusion if the revision is offered with genuine respect for the process of revision. **Current-Caller Nerida** is the most flexible of the three and the most dangerous to underestimate; she has been assembling coalitions since before the Empire's current political structure existed and will have already seen four moves ahead on any proposal the party brings.

### The Corruption Monitoring Station

*Eastern observation platform, forty feet above ground level*

The platform was built in a week, which shows, and it is built precisely well enough to do what it needs to do, which also shows. The Imperial engineers who built it have the look of people who are proud of specific things and cannot quite access the pride because of what the platform is for.

The monitoring apparatus is a collaboration that should not work and works imperfectly. Imperial calibration equipment. Verdant root-sensing filaments that extend into the corrupted soil half a mile distant and return data in the form of color and temperature rather than numbers. Covenant water-reading bowls that track the spread through underground aquifers. A junior ritualist from each faction is posted here at all times. They have developed, over three weeks of shared proximity and shared dread, a form of communication that none of their senior colleagues would fully recognize as professional.

What they can tell the party: **Imperial territory** is 60% corrupted — the corruption moves differently in the cities, finding infrastructure and following it, which accelerates the spread beyond what open-field projections would suggest. **Verdant territory** is 50% corrupted, and the forest is not dying passively; it is attempting to contain the spread, and the attempt is costing something. Theron knows. He has not shared the full accounting. **Covenant waters** are 45% corrupted — the sea's movement has been slowing the spread, but the corruption has been learning the currents. The last three Covenant readings show adaptation. The current rate of spread is 15% per week and accelerating. Critical mass — the threshold beyond which reversal becomes cosmologically uncertain — is estimated at four to six weeks.

The junior ritualists will give these numbers without being asked. They have been waiting for someone to give them to.

### The Equipment and Preparation Complex

*Supply depot, southern festival grounds*

Three warehouses built against each other, each organized according to principles that make perfect sense within themselves and require translation at every shared wall. An Imperial quartermaster has developed a functional hybrid notation system over the past week. She looks like she will never fully recover from having had to do this, and also like she is quietly proud of it.

Each faction has contributed what they know how to make. The **Empire** has provided entropy-resistant field gear — treated leather and layered metal alloys that slow the physical degradation that prolonged corruption exposure causes, alongside standard-issue communication crystals that are reliable and inflexible. The **Verdant Circle** has provided adaptive camouflage woven from cultivated moss-silk that reads the environment rather than imitating a fixed pattern, botanical reagents specific to each potential fragment site, and deep-root anchoring spikes that will hold a camp stable in temporal distortion. The **Ocean Covenant** has provided navigation tools that work on internal motion rather than external reference — essential in realms where landmarks do not behave — waterproofed charts that include intelligence beyond ordinary maps, and a selection of void-resistant amulets, crude but effective, each one marked with a different tide pattern.

Characters with specific proficiencies or backgrounds will find things in this complex that were prepared with them in mind — not because anyone knew them personally, but because **Nerida** has been running probability assessments on likely Pregenitor compositions since the second day.

## The Entropy Assault

The assault begins at the worst possible time, which is not coincidence. The **Entropy Aspect** has been watching the preparations and has chosen the moment when the war camp is most extended, most exposed, most full of civilians who arrived seeking sanctuary and did not sign on for what is about to happen. The sequence has three movements, and they are designed to dismantle three different kinds of cohesion.

**Phase One: The Personal Unraveling.** Before a single void creature manifests, before any physical threat becomes apparent, the Aspect reaches into the camp like a hand reaching into still water. Each person present receives something tailored. A vision, a voice, a sudden certainty. The party members receive the strongest versions — the Aspect has had three weeks to learn them. The visions are not random. They find the specific conviction that is carrying each character through the crisis and offer it back as a lie: *this is all futile, you know it is futile, you have always known*. Characters defined by hope are shown a future where the hope was misplaced. Characters defined by duty are shown a future where the duty was a trap. Characters defined by connection are shown versions of those connections breaking in ways that feel precise and inevitable.

The faction leaders are not immune. **Lyria's** confession — the twenty-two years of it — rises up as evidence for the prosecution. **Theron** does not speak for several minutes. **Nerida** is the first to recognize what is happening and name it, which breaks the Phase for herself and helps break it for others nearby, but the recognition costs her something that will not be visible until later. Characters may attempt DC 14 Wisdom saving throws to resist the compulsion of the visions; on a failure, they are Frightened until the end of their next turn. A character who has already broken free may spend their action to help an adjacent ally repeat the save.

**Phase Two: The Incursion.** The void spawns materialize in the gaps — the spaces between tent clusters, the paths between the faction sections, the supply depot's unlighted corners. Two dozen of them. They are not behaving like a raid; they are behaving like a search pattern, systematically working toward the equipment stores and the faction leaders' quarters. The **Entropy Field** extends two hundred feet from the Aspect's presence, suppressing restorative magic and imposing a cold blue tinge on light sources that makes the camp feel like it is already over.

This is where the alliance either coheres or fractures. Faction soldiers who have spent three weeks being professionally polite to each other are now in the same fight, taking fire from the same direction, covering the same wounded. The party has choices: secure the faction leaders, defend the equipment stores the expedition cannot depart without, protect the refugees sheltering in the ceremony tents at the camp's edge. The faction soldiers will follow the party's lead if the party projects the specific quality that military people recognize as *someone who has decided what to do and done it before*.

**Phase Three: The Cost and the Proof.** What the assault reveals — after the void spawns have been driven back, after the Entropy Field has collapsed or been pushed out, after the triage is underway — is two things simultaneously. The scope of what is opposing the alliance is not theoretical anymore. It came here, in force, with preparation and intent. That is the cost. The proof is what happened during the fight itself: Verdant root-barriers protected Imperial soldiers. Covenant navigation amulets were passed to children who were not Covenant. An Imperial communications crystal, slightly damaged, was used to coordinate a Verdant unit that had lost its grove-speaker. None of this was ordered. It happened because the need was there and the people were there. The faction leaders see this. They do not say much about it. They do not need to.

---

## Consequences

**Success:** The alliance holds — more than holds, it has been pressure-tested and found capable. The expedition departs with full faction support, maximum resources, and a unanimous target decision reached in the hour after the assault when everyone was too honest for politics. The war camp is damaged. Everyone in it is alive.

**Failure:** The void spawns reach the equipment stores and the expedition's capacity is severely degraded. The faction leaders, shaken and second-guessing, retreat toward their separate command structures. The party can still attempt the fragment recovery, but they go with what they can carry and without the coordination that makes the difference between an expedition and a raid.

**What the Party Now Knows:** The Entropy Aspect is not reacting to them. It is anticipating them. Somewhere in the understanding of that difference is the shape of what they are actually up against — not a force that needs to be defeated but a will that needs to be outthought. The assault was a test. The Aspect wanted to know what the alliance could do when pushed. The party needs to start thinking about what information they just handed over.
