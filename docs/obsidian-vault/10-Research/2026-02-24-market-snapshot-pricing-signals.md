# Market Snapshot: Pricing and Commercial Signals

Date: 2026-02-24

## What we verified (current sources)

1. D&D Beyond subscription pricing:
- Hero: `$2.99` monthly
- Master: `$5.99` monthly
- Longer terms discounted

2. Foundry pricing model:
- One-time license: `$50`
- No mandatory recurring tier for players

3. Roll20 pricing reference:
- Plus: `$5.99/mo`
- Pro: `$10.99/mo`
- Elite appears in current feature docs at higher tier pricing

4. Adjacent campaign/wiki tools:
- LegendKeeper: `$9/mo` (or `$90/yr`)
- World Anvil: paid tiers around low-mid single digit monthly into higher tiers

5. Demand-side macro signal:
- Hasbro Wizards & Digital Gaming segment continued growth in 2025 disclosures, indicating ongoing digital spend in tabletop-adjacent ecosystems.

## Implications for QuiverDM

1. Current QuiverDM pricing (`$9 Pro`, `$19 Team`) sits inside acceptable category anchors for serious DM tooling.
2. Free tier must demonstrate value quickly, but not subsidize heavy transcription/image costs.
3. Pro should map to active DM monthly cadence (multiple sessions/month).
4. Team pricing should be justified by collaboration + heavier limits, not only feature labels.

## Immediate research follow-ups

1. Validate price-package fit with 20-30 DM interviews and a preference test.
2. Add measured COGS-per-paid-user tracking before expanding free caps.
3. Benchmark retention for users who integrate Foundry sidecar vs non-integrated users.
