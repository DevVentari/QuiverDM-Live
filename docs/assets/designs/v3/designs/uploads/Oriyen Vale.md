## Background

Raised as a scholar within the Archive of Withered Echoes in the Shadowfell, Oriyen trained in secret with Kensei scrolls. He later created a personal path blending martial and shadow techniques: the Way of the Blinded Eye. Marked by visions and regret, Oriyen now seeks to intervene rather than observe. His companionship with Norm and Skreek opened him to the irrational, healing chaos of life.

## Downtime Activities

### The Archive's Shadow - Ongoing

Through visions revealed by Ambric at the Bonfire Keep, Oriyen has discovered that his archival work in the Shadowfell Archive of Withered Echoes attracted dangerous attention. His research into "The Bloodline Chronicles of House Aurelius" was observed by a **Knowledge Serpent**—a shapeshifting entity that feeds on dangerous information and uses it to breach into the material world.

**Key Discoveries:**
- A pattern of chroniclers working on imperial bloodlines experiencing supernatural harassment: Scribe Valdris (heard whispers), Chronicler Mesh'tar (increasing paranoia), Senior Archivist Kellam (vanished entirely)
- Oriyen's documentation may have revealed vulnerabilities in ancient magical protections tied to imperial bloodlines
- The entity appears to be preparing to act on this knowledge imminently
- Knowledge Serpents infiltrate through corrupted memories, intrusive dreams, and forged documentation

**Current Status:** Oriyen has learned the nature of the threat and understands it may attempt direct contact, but has not yet taken protective measures or completed investigation into the full scope of the breach.

**Character Growth:** Beginning to understand that his role as chronicler carries cosmic responsibility—his documentation can become a weapon in the wrong hands.

### Oriyen's Final Vision

As you polish your obsidian mirror in preparation for evening meditation, the surface doesn't reflect your face. Instead, you see through someone else's eyes—standing in Imperial Guard armor over an emperor's corpse. The dead ruler bears the Aurelius crest you know so well from your research. Your hands shake as you look down at the ceremonial sword, still wet with royal blood. 

But then you catch your reflection in a polished vault door, and the face staring back is unmistakably **Temmel's**—younger, but definitely the same weathered features, the same haunted eyes you see serving drinks each evening. The vision-Temmel is weeping, but not from guilt. From terrible understanding. 

Around him, strange magical apparatus hums with eldritch energy, and you hear him whisper: "I had to stop this... the power was consuming him... but was I right? Was there another way?" 

The vision fades, your mirror returning to normal obsidian black. But etched faintly in the surface, as if burned by what you witnessed, are the words: *"The bartender's burden spans centuries."*

**Final Character Growth:** Oriyen now understands that Temmel carries the weight of assassinating an Emperor Aurelius centuries ago, believing he was stopping a ruler corrupted by dark power, and that his documentation of imperial bloodlines has uncovered a pattern of cosmic significance spanning over a millennium.

# Fragment of Obsidian Mirror
_Wondrous Item, Artifact (Growing)_

## Physical Description

A jagged shard of black volcanic glass about the size of a palm, with razor-sharp edges that catch light like dark flames. The obsidian surface is perfectly polished despite its broken state, reflecting not just your face but glimpses of moments when you chose to watch rather than act. The shard is cold to the touch, almost uncomfortably so, and the scenes within shift constantly—showing what happened alongside ghostly overlays of what could have been. Golden light occasionally flickers deep within the black glass, like distant stars in an endless void.

When Oriyen holds the mirror fragment, he sees not just his own reflection but the weight of every moment he chose observation over intervention. The cold surface seems to burn his fingers with the chill of regret, yet within that painful clarity lies something precious—the ability to truly see the consequences of inaction, and perhaps, the wisdom to act differently next time.
